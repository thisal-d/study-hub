## 🎯 Introduction

> **"Corporate social responsibility is measured in terms of businesses improving conditions for their employees, shareholders, communities, and environment."**

- **Corporate Responsibility (ආයතනික සමාජ වගකීම — a company's commitment to act ethically and contribute positively to society, the environment, and its employees, beyond just making profit)** = ensuring organizations consider the social and ethical consequences of the technologies they create
- **Digital Inclusion (ඩිජිටල් ඇතුළත් කිරීම — ensuring that all individuals, regardless of disability, language, income, or location, can meaningfully access and use digital technology)** = no individual or community is left behind in the digital world

> 💡 **Real-World Example:** A bank launches a mobile banking app available only in English with no accessibility features. This automatically excludes rural communities, elderly users, and people with visual impairments — a failure of digital inclusion.

---

## 🏛️ Archie Carroll's Four Responsibilities of Business

**Archie B. Carroll** (University of Georgia) proposed that every business has **4 layered responsibilities**, arranged as a pyramid:

| **#** | **Responsibility** | **What It Means** | **Example** |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Economic (ආර්ථික වගකීම — a company's primary duty to produce valuable goods or services, generate profit, repay creditors, and grow shareholder wealth)** | Produce goods of value; repay creditors; increase shareholder wealth | A software company must make profit to remain viable |
| 2 | **Legal (නීතිමය වගකීම — obeying all laws and government regulations that apply to the business; this is the minimum required standard)** | Obey laws and regulations defined by governments | Following GDPR, PDPA, Computer Crimes Act |
| 3 | **Ethical (සදාචාරාත්මක වගකීම — following the generally accepted moral beliefs and social norms of society, even when not required by law)** | Follow generally accepted moral beliefs and social norms | Not discriminating in hiring; providing fair wages |
| 4 | **Discretionary / Philanthropic (ස්වේච්ඡා සමාජ වගකීම — purely voluntary acts of goodwill toward society that go beyond what ethics or law requires)** | Purely voluntary obligations; going above and beyond | Company provides a daycare center for employees' children |

> 💡 **Example:** A Sri Lankan telecom company:
> - **Economic:** Earns profit selling mobile plans
> - **Legal:** Follows TRCSL regulations
> - **Ethical:** Does not sell personal data without consent
> - **Discretionary:** Donates to rural school digital literacy programs

## 🌐 Why Corporate Responsibility & Digital Inclusion Matter

- **Technology reinforces existing social divisions** — digital inequalities in Sri Lanka mirror economic inequalities
- Corporations must act ethically when building and deploying digital systems
- **Equity (සාම්‍යභාවය — fairness in the distribution of opportunities, resources, and access; ensuring people get what they need, not just equal treatment)** must be a central goal of technology design

### Key Points:
- Digital inclusion ensures **no individual or community is left behind (ිෙ)**
- Aligns with **global sustainability (ගෝලීය තිරසාරභාවය)** and ethical governance
- In Sri Lanka, digital inequalities can reinforce existing **social and economic divisions (සමාජ හා ආර්ථික බෙදීම් — gaps between rich and poor, urban and rural, educated and uneducated)**

## 💻 Understanding Digital Inclusion

> **Definition:** *Ensuring everyone, regardless of age, gender, income, disability, or location, can access and effectively use digital technologies.*

Digital inclusion is **not just about internet access** — it is about meaningful participation:

| **Component** | **What It Means** |
|---|---|
| **Access (ප්‍රවේශය — having the physical infrastructure needed: internet connection, devices, and reliable connectivity)** | Availability of internet, devices, connectivity |
| **Affordability (දැරිය හැකි — the cost of devices and internet must be low enough that everyone can afford them)** | Cost barriers must be addressed |
| **Ability (ඩිජිටල් සාක්ෂරතාව — having the skills and knowledge to use digital technology effectively, such as using apps, emails, and online services)** | Digital literacy and skills |
| **Accessibility (ප්‍රවේශ්‍යතාව — designing technology so people with disabilities, such as those with visual or hearing impairments, can use it equally)** | Inclusive design for people with disabilities |
| **Awareness (දැනුවත් කිරීම — helping people understand the benefits of digital technology and how it can improve their lives)** | Understanding the benefits of digital technologies |

## ⚠️ Digital Divide Issues

> **Digital Divide (ඩිජිටල් හිඩැස — the gap between people who have access to digital technology and those who do not, often reflecting deeper social and economic inequalities)**

### Global Issues:
- **2.7 billion people** still offline worldwide
- Rural communities lack high-speed connectivity
- People with **disabilities (ශාරීරික-ඌනතා — physical or mental conditions that affect a person's ability to perform certain activities)** are often left out of tech design

### Sri Lankan Issues:

| **Issue** | **Impact** |
|---|---|
| Rural areas have lower internet and device penetration | People in remote areas miss out on digital services |
| Some government services lack **accessibility tools (ප්‍රවේශ-මෙවලම් — features that help people with disabilities use technology, such as screen readers or large text options)** | Citizens with disabilities cannot access services |
| **Digital literacy (ඩිජිටල් සාක්ෂරතාව)** gaps among senior citizens | Elderly people cannot use apps, online banking, etc. |
| Urban vs rural divide | Better services available only in Colombo and cities |
| English vs Sinhala/Tamil speakers | Platforms only in English exclude millions |

> 💡 **Example:** A farmer in rural Kurunegala cannot access agricultural market prices online because he has no smartphone, no English literacy, and no internet access. This is **digital exclusion (ඩිජිටල් ෙ — the state of being unable to participate in the digital world due to barriers of access, cost, skill, or language)** that corporate responsibility must address.

## 🏢 Corporate Responsibilities in IT

### Responsibility Toward Clients:
- Systems must **protect user data**
- Interfaces must be **easy to use (ues)**
- Services must **not exclude** people based on language, disability, or income
- Deliver **secure (ආරක්ෂිත — protected from unauthorized access, data theft, and system failure)**, accessible, inclusive systems
- Be **transparent (විනිවිද)** in data usage

### Responsibility Toward Employers:
- **Follow cybersecurity (සයිබර් ආරක්ෂාව — practices and technologies that protect computer systems and networks from unauthorized access, damage, or attacks)** and privacy policies
- Report **security vulnerabilities (ආරක්ෂක-දුර්වලතා — weaknesses in a system that could be exploited by hackers or malicious software)**
- Avoid misuse of company data
- Support sustainable and inclusive digital practices

### Responsibility Toward Colleagues:
- **Foster inclusive workplaces (ඇතුළත් කිරීමේ කාර්ය-ස්ථාන — working environments where every person feels respected, valued, and able to contribute fully)**
- Avoid **discrimination (වෙනස්කොට සැලකීම)** and support **belonging (අයිතිය)**

### Responsibility Toward Society:

| **Obligation** | **What It Means** |
|---|---|
| Create technologies that **uplift communities (ජනතාව ඉහළ නැංවීම — using technology to improve the lives of ordinary people, especially those who are disadvantaged)** | Serve people's real needs, not just profit |
| **Reduce digital divides** | Ensure underserved communities get access |
| Ensure **fairness, accessibility, sustainability** | Design for all ability levels and languages |

> 💡 **Example:** A Sri Lankan edtech startup builds a learning app that works **offline** (for students without internet), in **Sinhala and Tamil**, and with **screen reader support** — demonstrating all four levels of corporate responsibility.
