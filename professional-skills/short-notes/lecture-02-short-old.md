# Lecture 2: Reflective Practice, Values, Beliefs, Attitude and Character - Short Note

"Reflection turns experience into insight." - John C. Maxwell

## 1. Reflection and Reflective Practice (පරාවර්තනය සහ පරාවර්තන පුහුණුව)
- **Reflection (පරාවර්තනය):** The deliberate, purposeful revisiting of an experience to explore and extract learning. Simply having an experience is not enough; reflection is needed to learn from successes and mistakes.
- **Reflective Practice (පරාවර්තන පුහුණුව):** A learner's ability to engage with an experience, critically analyze it, and apply the learned lessons to future activities.

### Approaches to Learning (ඉගෙනුම් ප්‍රවේශයන්)
| Learning Type | Characteristics | Example / Scenario |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Surface Learning (මතුපිට ඉගෙනුම)** | Memorizes facts to just complete a task. No deep connection to existing knowledge. | Cramming for an exam just to pass, forgetting everything the next day. |
| **Deep Learning (ගැඹුරු ඉගෙනුම)** | Seeks true meaning. Connects new ideas to past experience and critically evaluates events. | Understanding *why* a code works and using that logic to build a whole new project. |

### 5 Levels of Achieving Expertise (විශේෂඥභාවය ළඟා කර ගැනීමේ මට්ටම් 5)
| Level | Name / Stage | Description | Example / Scenario |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1** | **Novice (නවකයා)** | High adherence to strict rules; no individual judgment. | A beginner developer blindly copying code from a tutorial without changing anything. |
| **Level 2** | **Advanced Beginner** | Gives equal importance to all aspects. Starting to learn. | A junior developer who knows basic syntax but treats every bug equally. |
| **Level 3** | **Competent Practitioner** | Able to perform standard, routine procedures well. | A developer who can comfortably build standard web pages on their own. |
| **Level 4** | **Proficient Practitioner** | Can easily differentiate between complex situations. | A senior developer spotting which part of a massive system is causing a unique error. |
| **Level 5** | **Expert Practitioner** | Has deep, implicit, unconscious understanding. | A tech lead who instantly designs a flawless architecture without needing a textbook. |

> **Real-World Example:** An **Expert Practitioner** (Level 5) in a hospital can look at a patient and instantly sense exactly what is wrong without even looking at their chart, whereas a **Novice** (Level 1) student will strictly follow a checklist item by item.

## 2. Models of Reflective Practice (පරාවර්තන පුහුණු ආකෘති)
To reflect properly, professionals use structured models.

### Gibb's Reflective Cycle (ගිබ්ස්ගේ පරාවර්තන චක්‍රය)
A cycle relying on answering helpful questions about an event:
1. **Description:** What happened? Where/When? Who was there?
2. **Feelings:** What were you feeling during and after? What were others feeling?
3. **Evaluation:** What was good and bad? What went well or did not go well?
4. **Analysis:** Why did things go well or badly? What sense can I make of it?
5. **Conclusion:** What did I learn? What else could I have done?
6. **Action Plan:** If it happens again, what will I do differently? How to improve skills?

### The DATA Model (DATA ආකෘතිය)
| Step | Meaning | Description | Example / Scenario |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **D** | **Describe (විස්තර කිරීම)** | Identify the area of practice needing improvement. | "My public speaking skills are very poor and I mumble." |
| **A** | **Analyse (විශ්ලේෂණය කිරීම)** | Find the factors causing the problem. | "I get highly nervous because I don't practice enough beforehand." |
| **T** | **Theorise (න්‍යායගත කිරීම)** | Suggest ways to improve and move forward. | "If I practice in front of a mirror 3 times, I will feel much more confident." |
| **A** | **Act (ක්‍රියා කිරීම)** | Try out the new theory to see how it works. | Practicing the speech in front of the mirror and then delivering it to the class. |

> **Real-World Example:** If a software release completely fails, the team will use the **DATA Model** or **Gibb's Cycle** in a "post-mortem" meeting to find out exactly why it failed (**Analyse**) and how to stop it next time (**Act**).

## 3. Personal Development Plan & SWOT Analysis (පුද්ගල සංවර්ධන සැලැස්ම සහ SWOT විශ්ලේෂණය)
**Personal Development Plan (PDP):** A strategic plan starting with a specific goal or a "gap analysis" (finding the gap between where you are and where you want to be).
*Example:* Improving BMI from 28 to 25 (Gap = 3 BMI points).

### SWOT Analysis (SWOT විශ්ලේෂණය)
A powerful tool to identify where you are strong and where the greatest opportunities lie.

| Category | Type | Description | Example / Scenario |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **S - Strengths (ශක්තීන්)** | Internal (Favorable) | Skills, experiences, and good qualities you already possess. | I am an excellent Java programmer. |
| **W - Weaknesses (දුර්වලතා)** | Internal (Unfavorable) | Things you do badly or need to improve upon. | I have very poor time management skills. |
| **O - Opportunities (අවස්ථා)** | External (Favorable) | Networking, trends, and resources available around you. | A major tech company is opening an office near my university. |
| **T - Threats (තර්ජන)** | External (Unfavorable) | External obstacles that could ruin your chances. | There is high competition for the same job from other graduates. |

> **Real-World Example:** A student wants an internship (**Goal**). Through a **SWOT Analysis**, they realize their coding is great (**Strength**) but communication is bad (**Weakness**). They decide to join a Toastmasters club (**Opportunity**) to fix the weakness so they don't fail the interview (**Threat**).

## 4. Values, Beliefs, Attitudes, and Character (වටිනාකම්, විශ්වාසයන්, ආකල්ප සහ චරිතය)

### Sequence of Character Development
1. Your **Values** shape your **Beliefs**.
2. Your **Beliefs** shape your **Attitudes**.
3. Your **Attitudes** shape your **Behaviors/Actions**.
4. Your **Behaviors/Actions** shape your **Habits**.
5. Your **Habits** shape your **Character**.
6. Your **Character** shapes your **Destiny**.

### Values (වටිනාකම්)
- Principles that help you decide what is right and wrong. 
- Once internalized, they act as a "compass" guiding your actions.
- Ex: Integrity (honesty), Kindness, Empathy, Justice, Learning, Health, Family, Freedom, etc.

### Beliefs (විශ්වාසයන්)
- Constructs we hold to be true, with or without actual proof/empirical evidence.
- E.g., Believing in a specific religion or believing hard work always leads to success.

### Attitudes (ආකල්ප)
- A mindset or tendency to act in a particular way based on experience and temperament.
- **3 Components:** Affect (feeling), Cognition (belief/thought), and Behavior (action).

| Attitude Type | Characteristics | Example / Scenario |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Positive Attitude (ධනාත්මක ආකල්ප)** | Optimistic. Pays attention to the good, focuses on solutions and opportunities. | Failing a test but thinking, "This is a great chance to learn my weak spots." |
| **Negative Attitude (ඍණාත්මක ආකල්ප)** | Pessimistic. Focuses on shortcomings, limitations, and the bad in situations. | Failing a test and thinking, "I am useless, I should just quit university entirely." |

### Character (චරිතය)
- Group of qualities making a person different from others.
- Good character means **knowing** the good (head), **desiring** the good (heart), and **doing** the good (hand).

> **Real-World Example:** A company will always hire an employee with excellent **Values** and a **Positive Attitude**, because they can easily teach technical skills, but they cannot easily teach someone how to be a good person.

## 5. The Johari Window (ජොහාරි කවුළුව)
A graphic model developed by Joseph Luft and Harrington Ingham (1955) to understand **self-awareness and interpersonal relations**. 

| Perspective | Known to Self (ඔබ දන්නා) | Not Known to Self (ඔබ නොදන්නා) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Known to Others (අන් අය දන්නා)** | **1. Arena (විවෘත ප්‍රදේශය):** Things both you and others know about you. (e.g., your name, hair color). | **2. Blind Spot (අන්ධ ප්‍රදේශය):** Things others see in you, but you don't realize. (e.g., you snore loudly). |
| **Not Known to Others (අන් අය නොදන්නා)** | **3. Façade (සැඟවුණු ප්‍රදේශය):** Secrets you know about yourself but hide from everyone else. | **4. Unknown (නොදන්නා ප්‍රදේශය):** Hidden talents or subconscious fears neither you nor others know yet. |

> **Real-World Example:** Asking your friends to give you honest feedback helps shrink your **Blind Spot** and expands your **Arena**, making you more self-aware.
