## 🎯 What is Leadership? (නායකත්වය යනු කුමක්ද?)

> **"Leadership is a function of knowing yourself, having a vision that is well communicated, building trust among colleagues, and taking effective action to realize your own leadership potential."**
> — *Prof. Warren Bennis*

- **Leadership (නායකත්වය — The ability to guide and influence a group of people toward a shared goal)** = Influencing others toward a common goal
- Leadership **cannot exist without followers** — great leaders need great followers
- Leadership style **depends on the environment** (calm ship captain vs. white-water rafter)

> 💡 **Real-World Example:** A calm company needs structured leadership (ship captain style), while a fast-changing startup needs flexible, adaptive leadership (white-water rafter style).

## 📚 Leadership Theories (නායකත්ව න්‍යායන්)

### 1️⃣ Trait Perspective (ගුණාංග දෘෂ්ටිකෝණය)

#### Great Man Theory
- Leaders are **born**, not made — they have **innate qualities (සහජ ගුණාංග — qualities a person is born with, not learned)**
- Originally focused on **male, military, Western** leadership

#### Trait Theory (Stogdill, 1974)

| **Traits (ගුණාංග)** | **Skills (කුසලතා)** |
|---|---|
| **Adaptable (අනුවර්තනීය — able to adjust easily to new or changing situations)** | Clever / Intelligent |
| Alert to social environment | Conceptually skilled |
| Ambitious & achievement-oriented | Creative |
| **Assertive (ස්ථිරව අදහස් ප්‍රකාශ කරන — speaking confidently and clearly without being rude)** | Diplomatic and tactful |
| **Cooperative (සහකාර — willing to work together with others toward a common goal)** | Fluent in speaking |
| **Decisive (තීරණාත්මක — able to make decisions quickly and confidently without hesitation)** | Knowledgeable about group task |
| **Dependable (විශ්වාසදායක — reliable; can be trusted to do what is promised)** | Organised (administrative ability) |
| Dominant (desire to influence others) | **Persuasive (ඒත්තු ගැන්වීමේ හැකියාව — the ability to convince others to agree with your idea or view)** |
| Energetic (high activity level) | Socially skilled |
| **Persistent (අධිෂ්ඨාන ශීලී — continuing to try even when things are difficult; not giving up)** | — |
| Self-confident | — |
| Tolerant of stress | — |
| Willing to assume responsibility | — |

Also key: **Integrity (අඛණ්ඩතාව — being honest and having strong moral principles; doing the right thing even when no one is watching)**, Honesty, Compassion, Humility

> 💡 **Example:** Don Stephen Senanayake — Sri Lanka's first PM — led the independence movement using adaptability, assertiveness, and deep responsibility.

### 2️⃣ Contingency Perspective (තත්ත්වාකූල නායකත්ව දෘෂ්ටිකෝණය)

> **Contingency (තත්ත්වය අනුව — means the right leadership style depends on the situation; there is no one-size-fits-all approach)**

**Hersey & Blanchard's Situational Leadership Theory (SLT)**
- Leadership style changes based on follower **competency (නිපුණතාව — skill and ability to do the job well)** and motivation

| **Competency** | **Motivation** | **Leadership Style** |
|---|---|---|
| Low competence | Low commitment (unable & unwilling) | 🔴 **DIRECTIVE** – Telling |
| Some competence | Variable commitment (unable but willing) | 🟡 **COACHING** – Selling |
| High competence | Variable commitment (able but unwilling) | 🟢 **SUPPORTIVE** – Participating |
| High competence | High commitment (able & willing) | 🔵 **DELEGATORY** – Observing |

> 💡 **Example:** A new employee (low competence, high enthusiasm) needs *Directive* leadership. An experienced but unmotivated worker needs *Supportive* leadership.

---

### 3️⃣ Transformational Perspective (පරිවර්තනීය නායකත්ව දෘෂ්ටිකෝණය)

> **Transformational (පරිවර්තනීය — causing a big positive change in people or organizations through inspiration, not just rewards or rules)**

**Bass & Avolio (1994)**
- Leaders **inspire** individuals, **develop trust**, and **encourage creativity**
- Followers develop a **sense of purpose** that goes beyond self-interest and simple reward

> 💡 **Example:** A tech leader who inspires their team to believe they are "changing the world" — motivating far beyond just salary.

## 🧠 Team Decision-Making (කණ්ඩායම් තීරණ ගැනීම)

> **"Team decision-making strategies should be used when you want to get participation and achieve consensus."**

### What is a Decision?
- **Decision (තීරණය — the act of making up your mind about something after careful thought and consideration)** = Choosing between alternative courses of action
- Involves: memory, thinking, **evaluation (ඇගැයීම — judging the value or quality of each option)**, choosing the best path

### Why Learn Decision-Making?
- To make **informed choices (දැනුවත් තේරීම් — choices based on real knowledge and analysis, not guessing)**
- To get everyone involved and **justify (සාධාරණීකරණය — to explain the reasons behind a decision clearly)** decisions to different audiences

---

## 🌍 The Systematic Decision-Making Process

| Step | What to Do | Example (Buying a Car) |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Create constructive environment | Define goal, involve right people, gather opinions | Involve family, define budget & needs |
| 2. Generate good alternatives | Brainstorm ideas from different perspectives | List sedan, SUV, hybrid, electric options |
| 3. Explore alternatives | Check risks, impacts, validate choices | Compare fuel cost, maintenance, warranty |
| 4. Choose the best alternative | Use grid analysis or decision trees | Score each car by cost, safety, comfort |
| 5. Check your decision | Test with own experience, check biases | Reflect on past car purchases |
| 6. Communicate & take action | Share the decision with reasons, then act | Negotiate price, complete purchase |

## 🏗️ Three Pillars of Decision-Making

1. **Environments** — Creating the right thinking space
2. **Methods** — How the team works together to decide
3. **Tools** — Practical instruments to assist decision-making

---

## 🌟 Features of a Thinking Environment (Nancy Kline)

| # | Feature | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Attention (අවධානය — listening carefully and genuinely to what others say without interrupting)** | Listen with respect and fascination |
| 2 | **Incisive Questions (තීෂ්ණ ප්‍රශ්න — sharp, direct questions that cut through assumptions and reveal the real issue)** | Remove limiting assumptions |
| 3 | **Equality (සමානාත්මතාව — treating everyone as equally important regardless of rank or role)** | Treat every member as a thinking peer |
| 4 | **Appreciation** | 5:1 ratio of appreciation to criticism |
| 5 | **Ease (සුවපහසු — relaxed, calm environment; urgency destroys creative thinking)** | Ease creates; urgency destroys |
| 6 | **Encouragement (දිරිගැන්වීම — supporting others to share ideas; moving from competition to collaboration)** | Collaborate, don't compete |
| 7 | **Feelings** | Allow emotional release to restore thinking |
| 8 | **Information** | Provide a fuller, more accurate picture of reality |
| 9 | **Place** | Create an environment that says "You matter" |
| 10 | **Diversity (විවිධත්වය — having people from different backgrounds, views, and skill sets adds quality)** | Differences improve decision quality |

## 🗳️ Methods of Decision Making

| # | Method | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Consensus (සාමූහික එකඟතාව — a decision that everyone in the group agrees with after open discussion)** | Enough time; high team commitment |
| 2 | **Majority Vote** | Time limited; moderately high commitment |
| 3 | **Minority Vote (EXCO)** | Very little time; small group decides |
| 4 | **Authority after group discussion** | Time allows talking but not full agreement |
| 5 | **Averaging individuals' opinions** | Limited time; low team commitment |
| 6 | **Decision by Expert** | Highly specialized knowledge required |
| 7 | **Authority without discussion** | Simple/routine decisions; very little time |

> 💡 **Example:** Emergency → Authority decides immediately (Method 7). New product strategy → full Consensus (Method 1).

## 🛠️ Tools for Decision Making

### 1. Brainstorming (අදහස් රැස්කිරීම — quickly generating many ideas in a group without judging any of them at first)
- Gather ideas **spontaneously** to solve a problem; no judgment during the session

### 2. Affinity Grouping (සම්බන්ධ කණ්ඩායම් ගැනීම — grouping related ideas together based on their natural connection)
- Group **unstructured ideas** from brainstorming based on natural relationships

### 3. Multi-Voting (බහු-ඡන්ද — each person gets multiple votes to narrow down too many options to the most important ones)
- When there are **too many options**, each member votes (1/3 or 1/2 of total options)
- Run multiple rounds until a **top priority list** emerges

### 4. Criteria Matrix (නිර්ණායක-ලකුණු — scoring each option against weighted criteria to find the objectively best choice)

| Criteria | Weighting | Option 1 | Option 2 | Option 3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Criteria 1 | 1 | 1 | 5 | 5 |
| Criteria 2 | 2 | 4 | 8 | 10 |
| Criteria 3 | 3 | 9 | 9 | 15 |
| **TOTAL** | — | **55** | **35** | **75** ✅ |

> **Option 3 wins** (highest weighted score = best decision)

### 5. Six Thinking Hats (Edward de Bono) — looking at a decision from 6 completely different perspectives, each represented by a colored hat

| Hat Color | Focus |
|---|---|
| ⬜ **White Hat** | Facts, data, information — *"What do we know?"* |
| 🔴 **Red Hat** | Feelings, hunches (අනුමාන — gut feeling without a clear logical reason), intuition |
| ⬛ **Black Hat** | Problems, risks, why it may NOT work |
| 🟡 **Yellow Hat** | Benefits, values, why it WILL work |
| 🔵 **Blue Hat** | Process management, next steps — controls the meeting |
| 🟢 **Green Hat** | Creativity (නව-නිපැයුම් — new and original ideas that haven't been tried before), alternatives |

> 💡 **Example:** Deciding on a new app launch — Black Hat: "what could go wrong?", Yellow Hat: "what are the benefits?", Green Hat: suggests creative new features.
