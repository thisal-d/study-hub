## 🎯 Key Terms

> **"The Code of Ethics and Professional Conduct is intended to provide guidance to help resolve ethical dilemmas that are likely to be confronted within the course of their employment."**

| **Term** | **Definition** |
|---|---|
| **Profession (වෘත්තිය — a job that requires specialized knowledge and long academic preparation, such as medicine or engineering)** | A calling requiring specialized knowledge and intensive academic preparation |
| **Professional (වෘත්තිකයා — a person who practises a learned profession and conforms to its technical and ethical standards)** | A person engaged in a learned profession, conforming to ethical and technical standards |
| **Professionalism (වෘත්තිකභාවය — the conduct, aims, and qualities that characterize a skilled, ethical professional in any field)** | The conduct, aims, or qualities that characterize a profession or professional person |

> 💡 **Example:** A software developer who maintains quality standards, communicates honestly with clients, and continuously updates their skills is demonstrating **professionalism**.

---

## 👤 Who is a Professional?

A professional:
- Has **Expert Knowledge (විශේෂඥ දැනුම — deep, specialized knowledge in a specific field that ordinary people do not have)**
- Has **Autonomy (ස්වාධිනත්වය — the freedom and independence to make professional decisions without constant supervision)**
- Has **special moral obligations** that exceed those of ordinary individuals

> 🔑 **Amateur (සෙල්ලම් කාරයා — someone who does an activity for fun or interest, not as a paid career with professional standards)** vs **Professional** — an amateur does it for fun; a professional does it as a career with accountability

---

## 🏛️ Attributes of a Profession

A true profession has:
1. A **professional body / association (වෘත්තීය සංගමය — an official organization like CSSL, IET, or IESL that sets standards and represents members of a profession)** (e.g., CSSL, IET, IESL, SLMC)
2. A **code of ethics** that members must follow
3. **Specialized, advanced knowledge**
4. **Service to the public interest**

---

## 💻 Who is a Computing Professional?

Anyone employed in computer, IT, or ICT fields:
- Software / Hardware designers
- Software Engineers, System Analysts, Quality Assurance
- Database Administrators, Technical Writers
- Software Managers, Computer Scientists

---

## ⚠️ Impact of Your Work

Computing professionals can have **direct life-threatening impact** through:
- ✈️ Aircraft and air traffic control systems
- 🚉 Mass transportation systems
- ☢️ Nuclear reactors and missile systems
- 🏥 Medical treatment systems

> 💡 **Example:** A bug in a hospital dosage-calculation program could give patients the wrong medication amount — a programmer's mistake could cost lives.

## 🧭 What is Ethics? (ආචාර ධර්ම — a set of beliefs and principles about right and wrong behaviour that guide how people act)

- **Ethics (ආචාර ධර්ම)** = A set of beliefs about **right or wrong behaviour**
- Ethical behaviour = behaviour that **conforms to generally accepted social norms**
- *Example:* Lying and cheating are **unethical**

### Why Ethics Matters:
- Helps people make **moral decisions (සදාචාරාත්මක තීරණ — decisions based on what is right and fair, not just what is convenient)**
- Assumes people are **rational (තාර්කික — capable of logical thinking and making informed choices)** and free to choose
- Describes **how people do act** AND **how people should act**

## 📜 Code of Ethics (ආචාර සංග්‍රහය — a formal written document that lists the ethical rules and standards that members of a professional body are expected to follow)

- **Professional Ethics** = personal, organizational, and corporate standards expected of professionals
- **"Computer Ethics"** includes social, political, and personal issues around technology

|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Professionalism (වෘත්තිකභාවය)** | Work to improve society; protect safety, welfare, and health |

## ⚙️ IEEE Codes of Ethics

- Accept **responsibility** in engineering decisions consistent with **safety and welfare**
- **Avoid conflicts of interest (ලාභ-ගැටුම් — situations where personal interests might interfere with professional responsibilities)** wherever possible
- Be **honest**; **reject bribery (අල්ලස — offering money or gifts to gain an unfair advantage)** in all forms

## 💻 ACM Code of Ethics

| **Principle** | **What It Means** |
|---|---|
| Contribute to society and human well-being | Your work should benefit people |
| Avoid harm to others | Do not create harmful systems |
| Be honest and trustworthy | Tell the truth; do not mislead |
| Be fair and do not **discriminate (වෙනස්කොට සැලකීම — treating people unfairly based on characteristics like gender, race, or religion)** | Treat everyone equally |
| Honour **intellectual property (බුද්ධිමය දේපළ — creations of the mind like software, inventions, or writing that are legally protected by copyright, patents, etc.)** | Respect copyright and patents |
| Respect **privacy (රහස්‍යතාව — the right of individuals to control who has access to their personal information)** | Protect personal information |
| Honour **confidentiality (රහසිගතභාවය — keeping sensitive information secret and not sharing it without permission)** | Keep sensitive information secret |

## 🎯 Five Functions of Professional Codes

Professional codes are designed to:

1. **Inspire (දිරිගැන්වීම)** — motivate ethical behaviour
2. **Educate (අධ්‍යාපනය)** — teach ethical responsibilities
3. **Guide (මඟ පෙන්වීම)** — help in making ethical decisions
4. **Responsibilities** — clarify duties and obligations
5. **Discipline (විනය — enforcing standards by removing or sanctioning members who violate the code)** — enforce standards

## ✅ IT Professionals Must Commit To...

| **Commitment** | **What It Means** |
|---|---|
| **Integrity (අඛණ්ඩතාව — being honest and morally consistent in all actions; doing the right thing even when no one is watching)** | Being honest and consistent in all actions |
| **Competence (නිපුණතාව)** | Working only in areas where qualified |
| **Professional Responsibility** | Upholding professional standards at all times |
| **Work Responsibility** | Delivering quality work to clients and employers |
| **Societal Responsibility (සමාජ වගකීම — considering the broader impact of your work on the public and society as a whole)** | Considering the impact of your work on society |

> 💡 **Ethical Scenario:**
> *You are a programmer at a bank and find flaws in an ATM program. Do you tell your employer?*
> ✅ **YES** — Report the bug and fix it. Hiding a faulty program is far more dangerous than admitting a mistake. **Do what is right, not what is easy.**
