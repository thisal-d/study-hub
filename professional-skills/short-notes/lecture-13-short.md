## 🎯 Why This Topic Matters

> **"Intellectual property (IP) is a category of property that includes intangible creations of the human intellect."**

- **IP (Intellectual Property / බුද්ධිමය දේපළ — creations of the mind, such as inventions, software, books, logos, and designs, that are legally owned by the creator and protected by law)** ensures creators are respected and rewarded
- **Innovation (නවෝත්පාදනය — creating something new or improving on existing ideas in a way that adds value)** must be ethical, inclusive, and socially responsible
- **Risk management (අවදානම් කළමනාකරණය — the process of identifying, analyzing, and reducing potential threats to an organization or system)** protects organizations, users, and society from digital harm

### Ethical Foundations:
- Respect **ownership (අයිතිකාරත්වය)** of digital creations
- Avoid **plagiarism (සාහිත්‍ය සොරකම — copying someone else's work and presenting it as your own without permission or acknowledgment)**, piracy, or unauthorized code reuse
- Anticipate the consequences of technological innovation
- Balance openness and creativity with responsibility

## 📖 What is Intellectual Property?

- **Intellectual Property (IP)** = Any **intangible asset (අදෘශ්‍ය වත්කම — something valuable that you cannot physically touch, such as a brand name, software, or a song)** that consists of human knowledge and ideas
- IP assets can be **owned, transferred, sold, or licensed**
- **Intent (අරමුණ):** to encourage ingenuity and protect creative ventures

### Types: `patents, copyrights, trade secrets, trademarks, contracts`

---

## 🗂️ Types of IP Protection

| **Type** | **What It Protects** | **Example** |
|---|---|---|
| **Patent (නව-නිර්මාණ ලේඛනය — a legal certificate that gives an inventor the exclusive right to use, sell, or license their invention for 20 years)** | Inventions | A new form of jet engine |
| **Trademark (වෙළඳ-නාමය — a word, name, symbol, or logo used to identify and distinguish a company's products from competitors)** | Brand identity | Logos, brand names |
| **Geographical Indications (GI) (භෞගෝලික ඇඟවීම — protection for products with a unique quality tied to their geographic origin)** | Products from a specific place | Champagne (France), Ceylon Tea |
| **Industrial Design (කාර්මික-නිර්මාණ — protection for the visual or ornamental aspects of a product, such as its shape or pattern)** | Ornamental or aesthetic aspects | 3D shapes, 2D patterns |
| **Copyright (ඇවිලීම-කිරීමේ අයිතිය — automatic protection for original creative works that prevents others from reproducing or distributing them without permission)** | Expression of ideas in a medium | Books, music, software, movies |
| **Trade Secret (වාණිජ-රහස — confidential business information that gives a company a competitive advantage and is kept secret from the public and competitors)** | Confidential information with commercial value | Coca-Cola formula, Google's algorithm |

> 💡 **Example:** In 2023, the Nugegoda Magistrate's Court ordered destruction of counterfeit **Polo Ralph Lauren** T-shirts and fined the seller Rs.50,000 — a real case of **trademark violation** in Sri Lanka.

## 🇱🇰 IP System of Sri Lanka

- **Intellectual Property Act, No. 36 of 2003** — the main law governing IP in Sri Lanka
- First patent in Sri Lanka: **November 22, 1860** (under the British Inventor's Ordinance of 1859)

**Act No.36 Covers:**
- Copyrights, related rights
- Patents
- Industrial designs
- Trademarks
- **Unfair competition (අසාධාරණ තරඟය — business practices that are dishonest or deceptive toward competitors, such as copying their products or trade secrets)**
- Geographical Indications
- Layout designs and integrated circuits

**Act No.36 Aims To:**
- Promote national creativity
- Protect national creative efforts
- **Facilitate (පහසු කිරීම — make easier)** and rationalize trade
- Protect consumer interests
- Integrate the national economy into the global knowledge economy

### International Treaties Sri Lanka has joined:
- Paris Convention (since 1952)
- Berne Convention (since 1959)
- Patent Co-operation Treaty (since 1982)
- **TRIPS Agreement (since 1995)** — Trade-Related Aspects of Intellectual Property Rights; sets minimum IP standards for all WTO member countries

## 🔬 What is a Patent?

> **"A legal certificate that gives you the right to protect your invention, for 20 years, in specific territories."**

- **Patent (නව-නිර්මාණ ලේඛනය)** = An **exclusive right (හිමිකම් ෙ — the sole legal right to use, sell, or licence an invention, preventing anyone else from doing so commercially)** to prevent or stop others from commercially exploiting your invention
- Protection starts **from the filing date** of the application

### Why File a Patent?
- **Exclusive control** in the market for your product
- Protection from **copying (copied — others cannot legally reproduce your invention)**
- Prevent competitors from developing **knockoffs (කොකපිය — cheap imitations of a product that copy the original)**
- **Licensing (බලපත්‍ර-දීම — giving another person or company permission to use your IP on agreed terms, while you retain ownership)** income
- Attracting investors and funding

---

## ✅ What Can Be Patented?

An invention must meet **3 principal requirements (Patentability Criteria — Article 27, TRIPS):**

| **Requirement** | **What It Means** |
|---|---|
| **Novelty (නව්‍යතාව — the invention must be completely new; it must not have been publicly disclosed anywhere in the world before the filing date)** | The invention must not have been publicly known before |
| **Inventive Step / Non-obvious (නව-සිතුවිල්ල — the invention must not be obvious to a person with normal knowledge in that field; it must involve creative thinking)** | Not obvious to someone skilled in that field |
| **Industrial Applicability (ිදිය හැකිකම — the invention must have a practical use and be applicable to industry, not just a theoretical idea)** | Must have a practical use; must be able to be made or used |

### Non-patentable subject matters:
- Mathematical formulae
- Laws of nature
- Naturally occurring organisms
- Abstract ideas, natural phenomena

---

## 📝 How to Apply for a Patent

| **Route** | **How** |
|---|---|
| **National (ජාතික — filing within Sri Lanka through the National Intellectual Property Office (NIPO))** | File with National Intellectual Property Office (NIPO) |
| **Regional** | File with a regional office serving multiple countries |
| **International (PCT — Patent Cooperation Treaty)** | File one application valid in multiple countries |

> ⚠️ **First-to-File Principle:** File the patent **before publicly disclosing** your invention. Once you present it at an exhibition, publish a research paper, or share it in an open forum, it becomes **prior art (ෙ-ශිල්ප — publicly known information that can stop you from getting a patent for something you thought was new)** and you may lose your rights.
> 
> 🇱🇰 **Sri Lanka has a 12-month grace period** — you can still file within 12 months after public disclosure.

## 📄 Licensing of IP

- **Licence (බලපත්‍රය — a permission that an IP owner grants to another person to use the IP on agreed terms, while retaining ownership)** = permission to use IP on agreed terms while retaining ownership
- **Licensing creates:** income source, legal framework for technology transfer, market presence
- **Software Piracy (මෘදුකාංග සොරකම — the unauthorized copying, distribution, or use of software without a proper licence)** = unauthorized duplication of copyrighted software

### Five Major Types of Software Piracy:

| **Type** | **What It Means** |
|---|---|
| **Soft-lifting (ෙ-ලිෆ්ටිං — buying one software licence but installing it on multiple computers beyond what the licence allows)** | Buying one copy but installing on multiple devices |
| **Client-Server Overuse** | More users than licences allow on a network |
| **Internet Piracy (ඉන්ටර්නෙට් සොරකම — downloading software from unofficial websites that do not have the right to distribute it)** | Downloading from unofficial sites |
| **Hard-Disk Loading** | Selling computers with unlicensed software pre-installed |
| **Software Counterfeiting (කොකපිය මෘදුකාංග — illegally reproducing and selling fake copies of software, packaged to look like the original product)** | Reproducing and selling fake copies of software |

---

## ⚖️ Fair Use (සාධාරණ-භාවිතය — limited legal use of copyrighted material without permission, under specific conditions)

**Copyright law allows limited copying under "Fair Use"** based on 4 standards:
1. **Purpose** of use — commercial vs non-profit educational
2. **Nature** of the copyrighted work
3. **Amount** of the work used relative to the whole
4. **Effect** on the potential market or value of the work

---

## 🔓 Open-Source Software (විවෘත-මූල-කේත — software whose source code is freely available for anyone to view, use, modify, and distribute)

- Source code is **freely distributed** and open for improvement
- Encourages examining and improving others' code
- New versions appear **much more frequently**
- Eliminates tension between copyright law and helping others
- Examples: **Apache web servers, Linux OS**

## 🔖 Key IP Types Compared

| **IP Type** | **Protects Against** | **Term** |
|---|---|---|
| **Trademark (10 years, renewable)** | Others using your mark; likelihood of confusion | 10 years, renewable |
| **Copyright (author's life + 70 years)** | Reproduction, distribution, derivative works | Author's life + 70 years |
| **Patent** | Commercial exploitation of your invention | 20 years from filing |
| **Trade Secret** | Misappropriation — NOT independent creation | No predefined limit (as long as secrecy is maintained) |

### Proprietary Licences (හිමිකාර-බලපත්‍ර — licences for commercial software where the publisher retains ownership and grants users only limited rights to use the software):
- Most commercial software is **licensed, not sold** — publisher retains ownership
- **EULA (End-User Licence Agreement — ෙ-ශිල්ප):** the legal agreement you accept when installing software

### Reverse Engineering (ආපසු-ඉංජිනේරු ශිල්ප — taking a finished product apart to understand how it works; in software, this usually means converting object code back into readable source code):
- Usually violates copyright unless **interoperability (ෙ — the ability of different systems to work together)** is the goal
- US and European law permit it in limited cases (e.g., to bypass a locking mechanism for interoperability)

## ⚠️ Risk Management in IT (IT-ගත අවදානම් කළමනාකරණය)

### Types of Risk:

| **Risk Type** | **Example** |
|---|---|
| **Cybersecurity risks (සයිබර් ආරක්ෂා-අවදානම — threats from hackers, malware, or data breaches that can damage systems or steal information)** | Hacking, data breaches, ransomware |
| **Legal/IP risks (නීතිමය-අවදානම)** | Using others' IP without licence; violating GDPR |
| **Ethical risks (ආචාර ධර්ම-අවදානම)** | AI bias, unfair algorithms, discriminatory systems |
| **Operational risks (කාර්යාත්මක-අවදානම — risks to the day-to-day running of a system or business due to errors, failures, or inadequate processes)** | System failures, process errors |

### Risk Management Process:

| **Step** | **What It Means** |
|---|---|
| **Identify (හඳුනා ගැනීම)** | Find all possible risks |
| **Analyse (විශ්ලේෂණය)** | Understand the impact and likelihood of each risk |
| **Mitigate (අවදානම් අඩු කිරීම — taking steps to reduce the probability or impact of a risk before it becomes a real problem)** | Take steps to reduce or eliminate risks |
| **Monitor (නිරීක්ෂණය)** | Continuously watch for new and existing risks |

### Sri Lankan Risks:
- Attacks on banks and telecom companies
- **Poor cybersecurity literacy (සයිබර් ආරක්ෂා-දැනුම-ඌනතාව)** among users and businesses
- **Startup vulnerabilities (ආරම්භ-ව්‍යාපාර දුර්වලතා — small new companies often lack resources for strong security)** in early-stage companies
