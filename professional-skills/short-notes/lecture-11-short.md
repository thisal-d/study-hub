## 🎯 Introduction

> **"Sustainability means meeting the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs."**

- **Sustainability (තිරසාරභාවය — using resources wisely today so that future generations can still meet their own needs; not causing long-term damage)** = the core principle of responsible development
- Technology **amplifies (විශාල කිරීම — makes bigger or more powerful)** human decisions at massive scale — small IT choices create large consequences
- **Ethical reflection (ආචාර ධර්ම-ගත ඒ-ප්‍රශ්නය — regularly thinking about whether your actions and decisions are morally right and responsible)** is essential because consequences are hard to predict

> 💡 **Real-World Example:** An energy-inefficient data center in Sri Lanka contributes to carbon emissions. A responsible IT company would choose green energy and energy-efficient servers to reduce its environmental footprint.

## 🌱 The Triple Bottom Line (ත්‍රිත්ව-ශේෂ — measuring a company's success not just by profit, but also by its social and environmental impact)

| **Pillar** | **Focus** | **IT Example** |
|---|---|---|
| **People (සමාජ)** | Social responsibility; fairness; inclusion | Building accessible apps for all users, including people with disabilities |
| **Planet (පරිසරය)** | Environmental responsibility | Using energy-efficient servers; reducing e-waste |
| **Profit (ආර්ථිකය)** | Economic responsibility | Sustainable business practices that generate long-term profit |

## 🔑 Key Themes

- Technology **disrupts (කඩාකප්පල් කරන — unexpectedly breaks or changes existing systems, industries, or habits)** privacy, security, work, and democratic systems
- **Social impacts (සමාජ බලපෑම)** — misinformation, automation, surveillance — must be carefully evaluated
- **Fairness, inclusion, and belonging** are **moral responsibilities (සදාචාරාත්මක වගකීම — ethical duties that professionals have toward other people and society)**
- IT decisions should consider **underrepresented voices (නියෝජනය නොලබන — groups that are often left out of decisions that affect them)** and social equity

## 💻 Ethics as a Framework for Responsible IT Practice

### 1. Why Ethics is Central to Computing

- Computing power enables actions (tracking, **profiling (පෞද්ගලිකභාව-විශ්ලේෂණය — collecting and analyzing data to build a detailed picture of a person's habits, preferences, or behavior)**, manipulation) never before possible
- Ethical dilemmas involve **balancing benefits vs risks (ප්‍රතිලාභ-අවදානම් සමතුලිතතාව)**
- Developers must **anticipate unintended consequences (හදිසි ප්‍රතිඵල — negative results that were not planned or expected when a technology was designed)**
- **Biased systems (අසාධාරණ-ව්‍යූහ — computer systems that produce unfair outcomes for certain groups because of flawed design or training data)** arise from biased cultures

| **Context** | **Examples** |
|---|---|
| **Sri Lanka** | Data leaks, national digital ID concerns, AI use in banking and HR |
| **Global** | Social media addiction, data exploitation, environmental damage from e-waste |

## 🌍 Sustainability in IT

### 1. Environmental Impact (පාරිසරික බලපෑම)

- **Rapid hardware obsolescence (ඉක්මනින් යල්පැනීම — technology becoming outdated very quickly, leading people to discard devices that still work)** → increases global e-waste
- Manufacturing electronics uses **hazardous materials (අනතුරුකාරී ද්‍රව්‍ය — dangerous chemicals and substances used in making devices that can harm people and the environment)**
- Data centers require **massive electricity** → contributing to **carbon emissions (කාබන් විමෝචන — greenhouse gases released by burning fossil fuels for electricity; a major driver of climate change)**

### 2. Global Sustainability Issues

| **Issue** | **Impact** |
|---|---|
| Hyperscale data centers (AWS, Google) | Very high energy demand |
| Software inefficiency | Unnecessary energy consumption |
| **Cryptocurrency mining (ක්‍රිප්ටොකරන්සි - ස්‌ථාපනය — the process of creating digital currency using computers, which requires enormous amounts of electricity)** | Extreme carbon footprint |
| Fast upgrade culture (phones every 1-2 years) | Creates massive **e-waste (ඉලෙක්ට්‍රොනික අපද්‍රව්‍ය — discarded electronic devices like old phones, computers, and batteries that are difficult to recycle safely)** |

### 3. Sri Lankan Sustainability Issues

| **Issue** | **Problem** |
|---|---|
| Rising e-waste | No robust **recycling infrastructure (නැවත-ප්‍රයෝජනය ගැනීමේ යටිතල — facilities and systems for safely recycling old electronic devices)**  |
| Cloud service adoption | Increasing energy demand |
| Government digital transformation push | More device usage → more waste |

### 4. Solutions

| **Solution** | **What It Means** |
|---|---|
| **Green computing (හරිත පරිගණකය — designing and using technology in a way that is energy-efficient and causes minimal environmental harm)** | Energy-efficient software and hardware |
| **Circular economy (කාර්ය-චක්‍ර ආර්ථිකය — an economic model where products are repaired, reused, and recycled instead of thrown away after single use)** | Repair, refurbish, and recycle instead of discarding |
| **Align with UN SDGs (UN ස්ථිර සංවර්ධන ඉලක්ක — 17 United Nations goals for a sustainable world by 2030, covering climate action, education, and equality)** | Climate action, responsible consumption |
| **Repair culture** | Fix devices instead of buying new ones every year |

## 🧑‍💼 Professional Responsibilities

### Summary Table

| **Responsibility To** | **What You Must Do** |
|---|---|
| **Clients** | Build safe, inclusive, reliable systems |
| **Employers** | Follow policies; report risks; uphold ethics |
| **Colleagues** | Support inclusive teamwork and equal opportunities |
| **Society** | Ensure tech contributes to social good, sustainability, and fairness |

### Core Ethical Obligations of IT Professionals:
- **Avoid harm (හානිය වළක්වා ගැනීම)**
- Be **honest and transparent (ස්පෂ්ට — open and truthful about what your system does and how it uses data)**
- Respect **privacy (රහස්‍යතාව)** and **confidentiality (රහසිගතභාවය — keeping private information secret)**
- Promote **fairness (සාම්‍යභාවය — treating all users and colleagues equally and without bias)**
- Prevent **discrimination** and **harmful bias ( හානිකාරී-පක්ෂපාතිත්වය — unfair favoritism built into a system that consistently disadvantages certain groups)**
- Encourage **psychological safety (මනෝවිද්‍යාත්මක ආරක්ෂාව — the feeling that it is safe to speak up, admit mistakes, or challenge ideas without fear of punishment or humiliation)**

## 🏛️ IT Laws Recap

### GDPR (EU):
- Companies must be **transparent (විනිවිද — open, clear, and honest about how decisions are made and data is used)**
- Users have **strong rights** over their data
- **Heavy fines (ශ්‍රේෂ්ඨ දඩ — very large financial penalties for breaking data protection rules)** encourage compliance

### Sri Lanka's PDPA:
- **Consent required** for data collection
- Users can request **deletion or correction**
- Organizations must **safeguard (ආරක්ෂා — protect from unauthorized access or harm)** all personal data

### Other Sri Lankan Acts:

| **Act** | **Purpose** |
|---|---|
| **Computer Crimes Act** | Illegal access and **hacking (හැකිං — breaking into a computer system without permission)** |
| **Electronic Transactions Act** | Digital signatures and online contracts |
| **RTI Act (Right to Information — ොදෙ)** | Transparency in government; citizens can request public data |

## 🏢 Corporate Accountability

- Companies must answer for **environmental, ethical, and social impact**
- They must **prevent harm**, not just fix it afterwards

**Real-World Examples:**
- **Google** → Running carbon-neutral data centers
- **Microsoft** → Accessibility-focused AI development
- **Sri Lankan companies** → Adopting green IT systems

**Tools for Ensuring Accountability:**
- **Audits (විශ්ලේෂණ — independent checks of a company's processes and records to ensure compliance with rules)**
- Sustainability reports
- Third-party reviews
- **Whistleblower protection (දූෂණ හෙළිදරව් ආරක්ෂාව — legal and cultural protection for employees who report unethical or illegal behaviour, so they do not face punishment for speaking up)**
