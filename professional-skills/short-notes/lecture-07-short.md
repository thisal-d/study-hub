## 🎯 What is Team Diversity? (කණ්ඩායම් විවිධත්වය යනු කුමක්ද?)

> **"Team diversity refers to the differences between individual members of a team that can exist on various dimensions like age, nationality, religious background, functional background or task skills, sexual orientation, and political preferences."**

- **Team Diversity (කණ්ඩායම් විවිධත්වය — the significant uniqueness of each individual on a team; the meaningful differences that each person brings)** = the significant uniqueness of each individual on a team
- It includes differences in **religion, gender, age, race, personality type**, and more
- Diversity is **not just visible differences** — it includes mindsets, values, and experiences

> 💡 **Real-World Example:** A software team with a young Gen-Z coder, a senior Millennial developer, a female UX designer, and a team lead from a different religion produces better, more inclusive software because of its multiple perspectives.

---

## 📋 What Does Diversity Include?

| **Category** | **Also Includes** |
|---|---|
| Religion | Introverts |
| Gender | Extroverts |
| Age | Liberals |
| Race | Conservatives |

> 💡 **Example:** An introverted team member may have great ideas but rarely speaks up in meetings. An inclusive team creates space for written input so all voices are heard.

## 🕌 Types of Diversity

### 1. Religious Diversity (ආගමික විවිධත්වය — differences in religious beliefs, practices, and values among team members)

- Brings **many viewpoints** in decision making
- The **most challenging (අභියෝගාත්මකතම)** diversity aspect to handle
- Managed effectively by:
  - **Training** all employees to understand company policy
  - Being **fair** in giving time for religious observations (prayer times, festivals)
  - **Encouraging** employees to accept differences
  - **Avoiding overreaction** to small things, but **not tolerating** serious issues

### 2. Gender Diversity (ස්ත්‍රී-පුරුෂ සමානාත්මතාවය — having both men and women fairly represented in the team and leadership)

**Benefits:**
- Widens the organization's **talent pool (දක්ෂ-සංචිතය — the range of skilled people available to hire)**
- Brings **multiple perspectives (බහු දෘෂ්ටිකෝණ)**
- Enhanced **collaboration (සහයෝගිතාව — working together effectively)**
- Improves **staff retention (කාර්යමණ්ඩල රඳවා ගැනීම — keeping good employees from leaving)**
- Better understanding of **customer needs**
- Improves organization's **reputation (කීර්තිය)**

**Challenges:**
- **Gender communication issues (ස්ත්‍රී-පුරුෂ සන්නිවේදන ගැටළු)** can affect team interactions
- Issues range from communication styles to **sexual harassment (ලිංගික හිරිහැර — unwanted sexual behaviour in the workplace)**

### 3. Age Diversity (වයස් විවිධත්වය — having team members of different age groups, each bringing different mindsets and experiences)

- Different generations bring **complementary strengths (අනුපූරක ශක்திmals — strengths that fill each other's gaps)**
- Increases **flexibility (නම්‍යශීලිත්වය)** and creates **well-rounded decisions**
- Members **mutually train each other**

| **Generation** | **Born Between** |
|---|---|
| Generation Alpha | 2013 – 2025 |
| Generation Z | 1997 – 2012 |
| Millennials | 1981 – 1996 |
| Generation X | 1965 – 1980 |

> 💡 **Example:** A Millennial manager (long-term planning experience) pairs with a Gen-Z developer (new tech skills, fresh ideas) → better outcomes together.

## 🌐 National Culture (ජාතික සංස්කෘතිය)

- **Culture (සංස්කෘතිය — the shared values, beliefs, and norms of behaviour that a group of people consider important)** = standards of social interaction, values, and beliefs from a group of people
- **National Culture** = the set of values and behaviours approved and passed on by a society over time

### Popular Dimensions of Culture

| **Dimension (මානය)** | **What It Means** |
|---|---|
| **Language (භාෂාව — the most visible part of culture; shapes how people think and express ideas)** | Most visible aspect; determines thinking |
| **Time Orientation (කාල දිශානතිය — how a culture views and manages time)** | Linear vs circular view of time |
| **Use of Space / Proxemics (ප්‍රොක්සෙමික්ස් — the study of how people use physical space to communicate; personal space varies across cultures)** | How much personal distance people need |
| **Religion (ආගම — influences ethics, behaviour, and economic decisions in a culture)** | Shapes moral codes and business conduct |

## 💬 Language: Low-Context vs High-Context Cultures

| **Type** | **What It Means** | **Examples** |
|---|---|---|
| **Low-context (සෘජු-සන්නිවේදන — the message is in the words themselves; communication is very direct and explicit)** | Words say exactly what is meant | USA (north), Canada, Germany, Scandinavia |
| **High-context (සංදර්භ-ගත සන්නිවේදන — words convey only part of the message; meaning depends heavily on context, tone, and relationship)** | Meaning is hidden in context | China, Japan, India, Sri Lanka, Arab countries |

> 💡 **Example:** In Germany (low-context), "No" means No directly. In Sri Lanka (high-context), a smile and silence might still mean "No" — the meaning is hidden in context.

## ⏰ Time Orientation

| **Type** | **View of Time** | **Characteristics** |
|---|---|---|
| **Polychronic (බහු-කාලීන — a flexible, circular view of time where relationships and the present moment matter more than deadlines)** | Circular | Present-focused; flexible; no immediate pressure |
| **Monochronic (ඒක-කාලීන — a linear, deadline-driven view of time where punctuality and planning are critical)** | Linear | Future-focused; deadline-driven; values planning |

## ⚠️ Challenges of Diversity

| **Challenge** | **Explanation** |
|---|---|
| **Discrimination (වෙනස්කොට සැලකීම — treating someone unfairly because of who they are, such as their gender, race, or religion)** | Unfair treatment based on identity |
| **Stereotyping (ජාති-ප්‍රකල්පනය — making fixed, oversimplified judgments about a person based on their group, not their actual abilities)** | Fixed oversimplified judgments |
| High turnover rate | Employees leave more often |
| **Absenteeism (නිතර නොපැමිණීම — frequently staying away from work because of discomfort or a hostile environment)** | Hostile workplace causes absences |
| Conflicts in team | Cultural misunderstandings cause disputes |
| Productivity decrease | Unmanaged diversity reduces output |

> 💡 **Example (Discrimination):** A qualified woman is passed over for promotion just because "the team prefers a male leader." This is unethical and often illegal.

### **Stereotyping (ජාති-ප්‍රකල්පනය — a widely held but oversimplified belief about all members of a certain group, often unfair and untrue)**
- We tend to **see what we expect to see** — *selective perception*
- Arises from **prejudice (පූර්ව-ග්‍රහය — having a negative opinion about someone before really knowing them, based on bias rather than facts)**

> 💡 **Example:** Assuming an older employee "doesn't understand technology" without giving them a chance ignores their actual abilities.

## 🤝 Inclusion and Belonging (ඇතුළත් කිරීම සහ අයිතිය)

> **"Inclusion is a sense of belonging. Inclusive cultures make people feel respected and valued for who they are as an individual or group."**

- **Inclusion (ඇතුළත් කිරීම — creating environments where every person feels respected, valued, and able to contribute equally)** = ensuring equal opportunities for contribution and success
- **Belonging (අයිතිය — the emotional feeling of being accepted, safe, and important within a group)** = the emotional outcome of being included; feeling safe and valued

### Inclusion in Team Models

| **Model** | **How Inclusion Helps** |
|---|---|
| **Tuckman's Stages** | Inclusion accelerates the Norming & Performing stages |
| **Belbin Roles** | Inclusive environments allow diverse roles to thrive |

**Benefits of Inclusion:**
- Encourages **open dialogue (විවෘත සංවාදය — honest, open conversation where everyone can speak freely)** and active listening
- Builds **psychological safety (මනෝවිද්‍යාත්මක ආරක්ෂාව — the feeling that you can speak up, ask questions, or make mistakes without being punished or judged)**
- Enhances **trust** and reduces conflict
- Leads to **better collaboration (සහකාර ක්‍රියාකාරීත්වය)**

> 💡 **Real-World Example:** A software team with both introverts and extroverts uses anonymous idea boards. This inclusion leads to better product ideas because no voice is left out.
