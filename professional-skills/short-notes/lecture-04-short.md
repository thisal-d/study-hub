# Lecture 4: Motivation, Time Management, and Goal Setting - Short Note

## 1. What is Motivation? (අභිප්‍රේරණය යනු කුමක්ද?)
- **Motivation (අභිප්‍රේරණය)** is a process that starts with a physiological or psychological **deficiency (ඌනතාවය)** or **need (අවශ්‍යතාවය)**.
- This need activates a **behavior (හැසිරීම)** or a **drive (තල්ලුව)** aimed at a **goal (ඉලක්කය)** or **incentive (දිරිගැන්වීම)**.

### The Motivation Cycle
| Component | Description | Example / Scenario |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Needs (අවශ්‍යතා)** | When the body or mind is deprived of something essential. | A person being deprived of food and water for hours. |
| **Drives / Motives (ප්‍රේරක)** | The internal tension that forces you to take action. | Feeling the hunger and thirst drives. |
| **Incentives (දිරිගැන්වීම්)** | The reward or goal that satisfies the initial need. | Finding and eating food, or drinking water. |

> **Real-World Example:** An employee struggling to pay bills has a financial **Need (අවශ්‍යතාව)**. This creates a **Drive (තල්ලුව)** where they work very hard to get a promotion. The promotion and the salary increment are the **Incentives (දිරිගැන්වීම්)** that satisfy their need.

## 2. Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs (මස්ලෝගේ අවශ්‍යතා ධූරාවලිය)
People fulfill lower-level needs before moving to higher-level needs.

| Level | Type of Need | Examples / Scenario |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1 (Lowest)** | **Basic Needs (මූලික අවශ්‍යතා)** | Needs like food, water, shelter, and basic pay. (E.g., A worker earning enough salary to buy groceries for the month). |
| **2** | **Security Needs (ආරක්ෂිත අවශ්‍යතා)** | Health insurance, pension plans, job security. (E.g., An employee getting permanent job status rather than temporary). |
| **3** | **Social Needs (සමාජ අවශ්‍යතා)** | Being part of formal/informal teams, having friends. (E.g., Joining a company sports team to build friendships). |
| **4** | **Esteem Needs (ගෞරව අවශ්‍යතා)** | Titles, status symbols, awards, promotions. (E.g., Being officially awarded "Employee of the Month"). |
| **5 (Highest)** | **Self-Actualization (ස්වයං-සාක්ෂාත්කරණය)** | Personal growth, realizing full potential. (E.g., A leader mastering their skills and leading a visionary new project). |

> **Real-World Example:** If a junior worker is worried about losing his job tomorrow (**Security Need**), he will not care about winning an office award (**Esteem Need**). He must feel secure before seeking recognition.

## 3. Herzberg's Two-Factor Theory of Motivation (හර්ස්බර්ග්ගේ ද්වි-සාධක අභිප්‍රේරණ න්‍යාය)
Job satisfaction and dissatisfaction come from two different sets of factors.

| Factor Type | Description | Example / Scenario |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hygiene Factors / Maintenance Factors (සෞඛ්‍යාරක්ෂක සාධක / නඩත්තු සාධක)** | Relates to work conditions that cause dissatisfaction if poorly managed. Improving them stops complaints but doesn't make people highly motivated. | **Examples:** Company policy, salary, security, working conditions. (E.g., Fixing a broken AC in the office stops complaints). |
| **Motivation Factors (අභිප්‍රේරණ සාධක)** | Relates to the psychological growth of an employee. Improving these leads to superior performance and extra effort. | **Examples:** Achievement, recognition, responsibility, growth. (E.g., Giving a developer a challenging, creative project leading to a big success). |

### Motivation-Hygiene Combinations (සංයෝජන)
| Combination | Resulting Worker Behavior | Example / Scenario |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **High Motivation + High Hygiene** | **High motivation, few complaints.** | Best situation: Workers are well-paid, happy with conditions, and doing engaging work. |
| **Low Motivation + High Hygiene** | **Low motivation, few complaints.** | Comfortably bored: Good salary and conditions, but the job is not challenging. |
| **High Motivation + Low Hygiene** | **High motivation, many complaints.** | Passionate but angry: The work is exciting, but the boss is bad or salary is very low. |
| **Low Motivation + Low Hygiene** | **Low motivation, many complaints.** | Worst situation: Boring work, bad salary, and terrible working conditions. |

> **Real-World Example:** Just increasing someone's basic salary (**Hygiene Factor**) will stop them from complaining about money, but it won't make them work significantly harder. To make them work harder, you must give them recognition and responsibilities (**Motivation Factor**).

## 4. Goal Setting (ඉලක්ක සැකසීම)
Effective people "Begin with the End in Mind." The Locky and Latham model shows that goal setting connects to performance and satisfaction.

### SMART Goals Characteristics
| Characteristic | Meaning | Example / Scenario |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **S - Specific (නිශ්චිත)** | Clear and well-defined. | "I want to get an A grade in Mathematics." |
| **M - Measurable (මැනිය හැකි)** | Can be tracked using numbers or data. | "I will score strictly above 85%." |
| **A - Achievable (ළඟා කරගත හැකි)** | Realistic to accomplish with your current resources. | "I will study 2 hours daily to achieve this." |
| **R - Result-based (ප්‍රතිඵල මත පදනම් වූ)** | Focuses on the final outcome, not just busywork. | "Passing the exam to secure my university degree." |
| **T - Time specific (කාලය නිශ්චිත කලා වූ)** | Has a strict deadline. | "I will achieve this by the final exam on December 15th." |

> **Real-World Example:** A vague goal is "I want to exercise more." A **SMART goal** is "I will run 3 kilometers (**Measurable & Specific**) every Monday and Wednesday morning (**Achievable & Result-based**) for the next 2 months (**Time specific**)."

## 5. Time Management (කාල කළමනාකරණය)
**Time Management (කාල කළමනාකරණය)** means paying attention to how you spend your most valuable resource (time) and creating a plan to use it effectively.

### Preparation for Time Management
- **Deep Night Time Sleep (ගැඹුරු රාත්‍රී නින්ද)** and balancing Life Time.
- **Balanced food, Water, Breathing, and Exercise (සමබර ආහාර, ජලය, හුස්ම ගැනීම සහ ව්‍යායාම)**.
- Organized Environment.
- **Mindfulness (සිහිය / මනසිකාරය)**.

### Task 1: Track Your Time (කාලය නිරීක්ෂණය කිරීම)
Calculate exactly where your 168 hours a week go by tracking sleep, grooming, meals, travel, chores, work, classes, and socializing.

> **Real-World Example:** Before someone can manage their time better, they should track everything they do for a week. They might realize they waste 15 hours a week playing games, which they can then redirect to studying.

## 6. Tools for Effective Time Management (කාලය කළමනාකරණය සඳහා මෙවලම්)

| Tool | Function | Example / Scenario |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **To-Do List (කළ යුතු දේ ලැයිස්තුව)** | Writing down tasks that must be done on paper or an app. | Writing down 5 important tasks every morning so you don't forget them. |
| **Time Tracking Apps (කාලය ලුහුබැඳීමේ යෙදුම්)** | Apps like **Toggl** allow you to track exact work/study hours. | Starting the Toggl timer when studying and stopping it during breaks. |
| **Time Management Matrix (කාල කළමනාකරණ න්‍යාසය)** | A grid sorting tasks by **Important (වැදගත්)** vs. **Urgent (හදිසි)**. | Dealing with an urgent client email first before scrolling social media. |
| **Pomodoro Technique (පොමඩෝරෝ තාක්ෂණය)** | Working in 25-minute intervals, separated by short breaks. | Setting a kitchen timer to 25 minutes to write a report without looking at the phone. |
| **Digital Wellbeing (ඩිජිටල් යහපැවැත්ම)** | Apps to minimize distractions like **Screen Time Labs** or **Android Focus Mode**. | Turning on Focus Mode to temporarily block Instagram notifications while working. |

> **Real-World Example:** A busy student uses a **To-Do List** to prioritize assignments. Then, they use the **Pomodoro Technique** (25 minutes studying, 5 minutes rest) to finish their work efficiently, while turning on **Digital Wellbeing Focus Mode** so their phone doesn't distract them.

## 7. Quick Revision Summary Table (කෙටි පුනරීක්ෂණ සාරාංශය)

| Topic | Key Points to Remember |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Motivation (අභිප්‍රේරණය)** | Starts with a **Need (අවශ්‍යතාව)**, creates a **Drive (තල්ලුව)**, and concludes with an **Incentive (දිරිගැන්වීම)**. |
| **Maslow's Needs** | 5 levels: Basic, Security, Social, Esteem, and Self-Actualization. Fulfill from bottom to top. |
| **Herzberg's Theory** | **Hygiene Factors (සෞඛ්‍යාරක්ෂක සාධක)** prevent unhappiness. **Motivation Factors (අභිප්‍රේරණ සාධක)** create true job satisfaction. |
| **SMART Goals** | Goals must be Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Result-based, and Time specific. |
| **Time Management** | Requires physical prep (sleep, exercise) and tracking the 168 hours of the week. |
| **Time Tools** | **Pomodoro**, Time Matrix, Toggl App, To-Do Lists, and Digital Wellbeing settings. |
