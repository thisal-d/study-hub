# Week 5: Teamwork and Team Dynamics

## 1. What is a Team? (කණ්ඩායමක් යනු කුමක්ද?)
- A **Team (කණ්ඩායම)** is a group of people who are **interdependent (එකිනෙකා මත යැපෙන)** with respect to information, resources, knowledge, and skills.
- They aim to combine their efforts to achieve a **common goal (පොදු අරමුණ)**.

### Team vs Group (කණ්ඩායම සහ සමූහය)
| Feature | Group (සමූහය) | Team (කණ්ඩායම) | Example / Scenario |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Performance (කාර්යසාධනය)** | Depends on what members do as **individuals (පුද්ගලයන්)**. | Includes individual results and **collective work products (සාමූහික වැඩ ප්‍රතිඵල)**. | **Group:** People waiting for a bus. <br>**Team:** A surgical team performing an operation together to save a life. |

### Characteristics of a Team
| Type of Team | Size / Duration | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Large Team** | 20-30 members | IT Department Lecturers, University Senate |
| **Small Team** | 2-5 members | Board of Directors, Surgeons doing a surgery |
| **Short Term Team** | Temporary | Event organizing committee |
| **Long Term Team** | Continuous | Parliament standing committee |

> **Real-World Example:** In a university, students put together for a one-day presentation form a **Short Term Team**, whereas the university Senate is a **Long Term** and **Large Team**.

## 2. Team Development Stages (කණ්ඩායම් සංවර්ධන අදියර)
According to **Dr. Bruce Tuckman (1965)**, teams go through **five stages (අදියර පහක්)** of development.

| Stage | Description | Example / Scenario |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Forming (සැකසීම)** | The team acquaints and establishes **ground rules (මූලික නීති)**. Formalities are preserved, and members are treated as strangers. | New students meeting on the first day of a university group project, politely introducing themselves. |
| **2. Storming (ගැටුම් ඇතිවීම)** | Members communicate feelings but view themselves as individuals. They resist control and may show **hostility (සතුරුකම/විරෝධය)**. | Members arguing about who will be the leader or what research topic to choose. |
| **3. Norming (ප්‍රමිතිගත වීම)** | People feel part of the team. They realize they can achieve work if they accept other viewpoints. | The group agrees on rules, assigns work to everyone, and starts cooperating. |
| **4. Performing (කාර්යසාධනය)** | The team works in an open, trusting atmosphere. **Flexibility (නම්‍යශීලී බව)** is key; hierarchy is less important. | Everyone is working hard together without conflict, helping each other to finish the report on time. |
| **5. Adjourning (විසුරුවා හැරීම)** | The team conducts an assessment of the work done, recognizes members' contributions, and disbands. | The project is submitted, the team celebrates their success, and members move on to other classes. |

> **Real-World Example:** When creating a completely new software project team, the members might initially clash over which programming language to use (**Storming**). Once they agree to use Java (**Norming**), they can successfully build the application together (**Performing**).

## 3. Team Roles (කණ්ඩායම් කාර්යභාරයන්)
A **Role (කාර්යභාරය)** is a set of behaviors required or expected of a person occupying a certain position in a group.

- **Formal Role (විධිමත් කාර්යභාරය):** Dictated by the organization's structure (e.g., Coach, Team Captain).
- **Informal Role (අවිධිමත් කාර්යභාරය):** Evolves naturally from the group's dynamics.
- **Role Acceptance (කාර්යභාරය පිළිගැනීම):** Important to enhance the group's structure.

### Belbin's Team Role Theory (බෙල්බින්ගේ කණ්ඩායම් කාර්යභාර න්‍යාය)
**Dr. R. Meredith Belbin** identified **9 Team Roles**, grouped into 3 main categories. A team role is defined as *"A tendency to behave, contribute and interrelate with others in a particular way."*

| Category | Role | Characteristics | Example / Scenario |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Action Oriented (ක්‍රියාකාරීත්වය මත පදනම් වූ)** | **1. Shaper** | Sees obstacles as challenges, pushes things, extroverted, stimulates others. | The team leader who pushes everyone to meet the tight deadline. |
| | **2. Implementer** | Disciplined, efficient, turns ideas into practical actions. (Can be inflexible). | The person who creates the step-by-step project schedule and sticks to it strictly. |
| | **3. Completer Finisher** | Perfectionist, works systematically, finds errors. (Reluctant to delegate). | The person who double-checks the final document for tiny spelling mistakes. |
| **People Oriented (පුද්ගලයන් මත පදනම් වූ)** | **4. Co-ordinator** | Supportive, caring, flexible, good negotiator. | The leader who organizes tasks and smoothly resolves a fight between two members. |
| | **5. Teamworker** | Diplomatic, popular, keeps team spirit high. (Poor at difficult decisions). | Uses humor and empathy to keep everyone happy during stressful, busy working times. |
| | **6. Resource Investigator** | Extroverted, overly optimistic, finds external resources. | The person who goes out and finds external sponsors or specific tools for the project. |
| **Thought Oriented (චින්තනය මත පදනම් වූ)** | **7. Plant** | Innovator, brings new concepts, introverted. (Can be impractical / poor communicator). | The creative genius who comes up with a unique, out-of-the-box system design. |
| | **8. Monitor Evaluator** | Critical thinker, carefully weighs pros & cons, unemotional to others. | Analyzes the Plant's creative idea to see if it's practically possible and cost-effective. |
| | **9. Specialist** | Single-minded, professional, has rare knowledge/skills. | The database expert who only handles the highly complex SQL queries. |

> **Real-World Example:** A movie production team has an imaginative director (**Plant**), an organized producer (**Implementer**), and an enthusiastic PR manager finding sponsors outside (**Resource Investigator**).

## 4. Problems with Roles (කාර්යභාරයන් සමඟ ඇතිවන ගැටලු)
Sometimes, team roles can cause dysfunctions within the team. 

| Problem | Description | Example / Scenario |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Role Overload (කාර්යභාරය අධික වීම)** | Taking on too much work in less time. Leads to stress, isolation, and dropped productivity. | A single student unexpectedly trying to do the research, writing, and presentation slides all alone. |
| **Role Conflict (කාර්යභාර ගැටුම)** | Different expectations of responsibilities, or a clash of multiple roles combined (**Inter role conflict**). | E.g., Sri Lanka's former President Maithripala Sirisena experienced Role Conflict by holding the presidency while also being Chairman of the SLFP opposition party. |
| **Role Ambiguity (කාර්යභාරයේ අපැහැදිලිතාවය)** | A role is too vague or undefined. The person doesn't know what is expected of them by the group. | A new employee is hired to a software team but hasn't been given a clear job description, causing them to isolate and lose confidence. |
| **Rigidity (දැඩි බව)** | Refusing to be flexible and strictly sticking to one expected role. | A frontend programmer refusing to help basic testing because "it's not my job." |
| **Abdication (වගකීම් අත්හැරීම)** | Relinquishing duties. Avoiding contribution because you think it's someone else's responsibility. | Seeing a critical bug in the final document but ignoring it entirely. |
| **Territoriality (බලප්‍රදේශය ආරක්ෂා කිරීම)** | Guarding personal space/role; reluctant to let others contribute to your assigned task. | A designer getting extremely angry when another member suggests a good color change for a button. |
| **Human Nature (මිනිස් ස්වභාවය)** | Unpredictability, straying from expected roles, inconsistency in behavior. | A usually calm, coordinated member suddenly yelling and acting aggressively due to a bad day. |

### Handling Role Problems
- Have **solid, unemotional discussions (සාකච්ඡා කිරීම)**.
- **Role re-delegation (කාර්යභාරයන් නැවත පැවරීම)** until responsibilities settle on the correct person.
- Use **Self-evaluation** and **Personality testing (පෞරුෂ පරීක්ෂණ)**.

### Alternatives to the Role Concept
- **Empowering (බලගැන්වීම)** team members.
- Focusing on **Multiskilling (බහු-කුසලතා සංවර්ධනය)** so people become versatile all-rounders.

> **Real-World Example:** If a team faces **Role Overload** because one developer is writing all the backend code, the manager can solve it through **Role re-delegation**, dividing the tasks equally among three developers.

## 5. Successful Teams and Teamwork (සාර්ථක කණ්ඩායම් සහ කණ්ඩායම් වැඩ)

**Teamwork (කණ්ඩායම් වැඩ)** is the cooperation between members with **diverse skills (විවිධ කුසලතා)** and backgrounds to achieve a common goal. It is the fuel that allows common people to attain uncommon results.

### Features of a Successful Team:
- Works together toward common goals.
- Perceives the relevance of group tasks.
- **Mutual respect (අන්‍යෝන්‍ය ගෞරවය)** for all members.
- **Diversity (විවිධත්වය)** of skills/experience.
- Respects individual **communication styles (සන්නිවේදන රටාවන්)**.

### The Dysfunctions of Groups and Teams (අක්‍රියතා සහ දුර්වලතා):
- **Groupthink (කණ්ඩායම් චින්තනය):** When team members try too hard to agree with each other, they stop thinking critically. They make poor or irrational decisions just to maintain team harmony and avoid conflict.

> **Real-World Example:** A management team might agree to launch a clearly flawed product just because the authoritative CEO suggested it, and no one wanted to disagree and cause a fight. This is a classic, dangerous example of **Groupthink**.
