## 🟥 Q1 — Leadership & Team Decision-Making (L6)

### Leadership Theories
- **Trait Theory** → Leaders have specific traits: adaptive, assertive, decisive, persistent, self-confident + skills: intelligent, creative, diplomatic, persuasive
- **Situational/Contingency (Hersey-Blanchard)** → Style matches follower readiness:

| Follower State | Style |
|---|---|
| Low competence + Low motivation | **DIRECTIVE (Telling)** |
| Some competence + High motivation | **COACHING (Selling)** |
| High competence + Low motivation | **SUPPORTIVE (Participating)** |
| High competence + High motivation | **DELEGATORY (Observing)** |

- **Transformational (Bass & Avolio, 1994)** → Inspire individuals, build trust, encourage creativity & personal growth beyond self-interest

### Bennis Quotes (use in essays)
> *"Leadership is a function of knowing yourself, having a vision that is well communicated, building trust among colleagues, and taking effective action."*
> *"Leadership cannot exist without the full inclusion, initiatives, and cooperation of employees."*

### Team Decision-Making: 3 Areas
1. **Environments** → Thinking Environment (10 features — key ones: Attention, Equality, Appreciation 5:1, Ease, Encouragement, Diversity)
2. **Methods** → Consensus → Majority Vote → Minority Vote → Authority after discussion → Averaging → Expert → Authority without discussion
3. **Tools** → Brainstorming → Affinity Grouping → Multi-voting → Criteria Matrix → **Six Thinking Hats**

### Six Hats
⬜White=Data | 🔴Red=Feelings | ⬛Black=Risks | 🟡Yellow=Benefits | 🔵Blue=Process | 🟢Green=Creativity

### Q1 Case Study (NextGen Lanka) Key Points
- **Problems:** Sanduni decides alone → no meetings → juniors excluded → weak collaboration → low motivation → delays
- **Leadership Issue:** Authoritarian/directive when she should use Supportive/Participative
- **Fix:** Structured meetings, consensus decision-making, open dialogue, use Brainstorming + Criteria Matrix
- **Quote for D-part:** *"Team decision-making strategies should be used when you want to get participation and achieve consensus"*

## 🟧 Q2 — Ethics, Diversity & Inclusion (L9, L7)

### Professional Ethics Definitions
- **Ethics** = beliefs about right/wrong behavior; conforms to universally accepted social norms
- **Professional** = expert knowledge + autonomy + special moral responsibilities
- **Code of Ethics** = formal document covering responsibilities to employees, employers, users, and the public

### 5 Functions of Code of Ethics
**Inspire | Educate | Guide | Responsibilities | Discipline**

### Key Professional Bodies (IT)
- **CSSL** → Professionalism, Development, Honesty, Competence, Value to Sri Lanka
- **ACM** → Public wellbeing, Avoid harm, Honest, Fair, Respect privacy, Honour IP
- **IEEE** → Safety, avoid conflicts of interest, be honest, reject bribery
- **ACM-SE (8 Principles)** → Public, Client/Employer, Product, Judgment, Management, Profession, Colleagues, Self

### IT Professional Commitments
Integrity | Competence | Professional Responsibility | Work Responsibility | Societal Responsibility

### Team Diversity Definition
> *"Team diversity refers to differences between individual members on dimensions like age, nationality, religion, gender, task skills, sexual orientation, and political preferences."*

### Benefits of Diversity (5)
1. Talents, skills & experiences
2. Creates innovation
3. Language skills open business doors
4. Grows talent pool
5. Improves employee performance

### Challenges of Diversity
Discrimination | Stereotyping | High turnover | Absenteeism | Conflict | Tension | Productivity decrease

### Inclusion Definition
> *"Inclusion is a sense of belonging. Inclusive cultures make people feel respected and valued for who they are as an individual or group."*

### Impact of LACK of Inclusion
| Area | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **Motivation** | Disengagement, low morale, reduced effort, alienation |
| **Teamwork** | Weak collaboration, silos, poor group dynamics |
| **Communication** | Distrust, avoidance, information hoarding |

### How Diversity Improves Creativity & Decision-Making
- Multiple perspectives → better ideas
- Wider knowledge pool → more informed decisions
- Religious/age/cultural differences → richer debate
- **Negative:** Communication barriers, conflict potential, coordination difficulties

### Applying Inclusion (D-part of Q2)
- Create psychological safety
- Encourage open dialogue & active listening
- Tuckman: inclusion accelerates Norming & Performing stages
- Belbin: diverse roles thrive in inclusive environments
- Impact: higher engagement, productivity, reduced turnover, stronger trust

## 🟨 Q3 — Data Privacy, Legal Responsibility & IP (L10, L13)

### Data Privacy Definition
> *"Accountability underpins all principles — taking responsibility, having measures in place, and keeping records to demonstrate data protection compliance."*

- Personal data = any info that can identify an individual
- **Importance:** Prevents misuse, builds trust, protects rights, ensures compliance with law

### GDPR (Key Points)
- EU law (2018); covers 447M people
- Applies to **any company** handling EU citizens' data (including Sri Lankan companies!)
- Fines: **€20M OR 4% of global revenue** (whichever is higher)
- Google €50M | Amazon €746M fined

### GDPR User Rights
Right to Access | Right to Correction | **Right to Be Forgotten** | Right to Data Portability

### Data Protection Principles (for D-part)
**Collect → Store → Use → Share → Delete**
**Consent → Security → Purpose → Transparency → Right to Erase**

### Sri Lankan PDPA (No. 9 of 2022)
- Consent required before collecting data
- Users can request correction or deletion
- Organizations must secure personal data
- Data Protection Authority enforces rules

### Other Sri Lankan Acts
- Computer Crimes Act (2007) → hacking offences
- Electronic Transactions Act (2006) → digital signatures
- Right to Information Act (2016) → public data access

### Two Legal Responsibilities (Q3b answers)
1. **Obtain Consent** — organizations must get explicit consent before collecting personal data (PDPA/GDPR)
2. **Ensure Security** — must implement technical safeguards (encryption, access controls) to protect stored data

### Intellectual Property Types
| Type | Protects | Duration |
|------|---------|----------|
| Patent | Inventions | 20 years |
| Copyright | Expression of ideas | Life + 70 years |
| Trademark | Brand names/logos | 10 years (renewable) |
| Trade Secret | Confidential commercial info | Indefinite |

### Patent Requirements (TRIPS Art. 27)
**Novelty + Inventive Step + Industrial Applicability**

### 5 Types of Software Piracy
1. Soft-lifting (multi-install from one copy)
2. Client-Server Overuse (exceed license count)
3. Internet Piracy (unofficial download)
4. Hard-Disk Loading (pre-install on sold PCs)
5. Software Counterfeiting (illegal reproduction)

### IP Risks Without Permission (Q3c)
- **Organizations:** Lawsuits, injunctions, fines, reputational damage, product recalls
- **Individual Developers:** Criminal liability, job termination, fines, long-term career damage

### Sri Lankan IP: Act No. 36 of 2003
Covers: copyrights, patents, designs, trademarks, trade secrets, geographical indications

## 🟩 Q4 — Sustainability, CSR & Digital Inclusion (L11, L12)

### Sustainability Definition
> *"Meeting the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs."*

### Sustainability in IT = Triple Bottom Line
**People (Social) + Planet (Environment) + Profit (Economic)**

### Two Environmental Issues in IT (Q4b)
1. **E-Waste** — rapid hardware obsolescence; hazardous materials; poor recycling infrastructure
   - **Fix:** Circular economy (repair/refurbish/recycle), extended product lifespans, government e-waste regulations
2. **Carbon Emissions / Energy** — data centers (AWS, Google), cryptocurrency mining use massive electricity
   - **Fix:** Green computing, renewable energy for data centers, energy-efficient software

### CSR — Carroll's Four Responsibilities
| Level | Type | Example |
|-------|------|---------|
| 1 | **Economic** | Produce goods of value, increase shareholder wealth |
| 2 | **Legal** | Obey laws set by government |
| 3 | **Ethical** | Follow generally held beliefs about behavior |
| 4 | **Discretionary** | Voluntary obligations (e.g., daycare, donations) |

### Digital Inclusion Definition
> *"Ensuring everyone, regardless of age, gender, income, disability, or location, can access and effectively use digital technologies."*

### 5 Components of Digital Inclusion
**Access + Affordability + Ability + Accessibility + Awareness**

### Digital Divide in Sri Lanka
- Urban vs Rural (internet/device gap)
- English vs Sinhala/Tamil speakers
- Young vs Elderly population
- 2.7 billion people still offline globally

### Impact of Lack of Digital Inclusion (Q4c — 2 key challenges)
1. **Educational Exclusion** — no access to e-learning, digital resources, online coursework → widens knowledge gap
2. **Economic Exclusion** — cannot access digital banking, e-commerce, government services, remote jobs → poverty cycle deepens

### Three CSR Initiatives (Q4d template answers)
1. **Digital Literacy Programs** — train rural and elderly populations → Benefit: bridges skills gap, empowers citizens economically
2. **Accessible Platform Design** — include screen readers, multi-language support (Sinhala/Tamil), voice interfaces → Benefit: includes disabled users and non-English speakers
3. **Subsidized Device/Internet Access** — partner with government/NGOs to provide affordable connectivity → Benefit: enables economic and educational participation for low-income communities

## ⚡ Extra Power Tips

### 5 Powers (French & Raven) — for MCQs
- **Reward** = control over rewards | **Legitimate** = positional authority | **Coercive** = threaten/punish
- **Expert** = has knowledge others need | **Referent/Charismatic** = personal attraction/admiration

### Ethical Models — Quick Apply
- **Utilitarianism** → "Benefits the most people" (e.g., pandemic tracking app — deploy with safeguards)
- **Deontology** → "Privacy violation is ALWAYS wrong" (e.g., secret employee monitoring = wrong even if productive)
- **Stakeholder Analysis** → "Consider ALL affected groups including minorities and vulnerable users"

### Common Case Studies in Exam
| Case | Lesson |
|------|--------|
| Cambridge Analytica | Consent violation, data privacy, GDPR |
| AI Hiring Bias | Gender discrimination, biased datasets, stakeholder analysis |
| NDB Bank Fraud | Weak internal controls, legal accountability |

### Writing Framework for D-type (Proposal) Questions
1. **Identify the problem** clearly
2. **State the principle** (CSR/inclusion/ethics)
3. **Describe the specific initiative/action**
4. **Explain the benefit** (who benefits and how)
5. **Connect to course concepts** (Tuckman, Carroll, PDPA, etc.)
