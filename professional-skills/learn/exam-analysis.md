### 1. Leadership & Team Decision-Making (Lecture 6) — Q1

**Why critical:** Q1 is explicitly built from this lecture, including the exact case study.

#### Leadership Definitions & Theories

| Theory | Key Idea | Exam Relevance |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| **Great Man Theory** | Leaders are born, not made (innate qualities) | Background context |
| **Trait Theory** | Specific traits (decisive, self-confident, persistent) + skills | MCQ, short answer |
| **Situational/Contingency (Hersey-Blanchard)** | Style matches follower *readiness* (competence + motivation) | **🔴 Very likely essay** |
| **Transformational Theory (Bass & Avolio, 1994)** | Inspire, build trust, encourage growth beyond self-interest | **🔴 Very likely essay** |

#### Hersey-Blanchard Situational Leadership — MEMORIZE THIS TABLE

| Competence | Motivation | Leadership Style |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Low | Low/Unwilling | **DIRECTIVE (Telling)** |
| Some | High/Willing | **COACHING (Selling)** |
| High | Variable/Unwilling | **SUPPORTIVE (Participating)** |
| High | High/Committed | **DELEGATORY (Observing)** |

#### Leadership Definition (Key Quote)
> *"Leadership is a function of knowing yourself, having a vision that is well communicated, building trust among colleagues, and taking effective action to realize your own leadership potential."* — Prof. Warren Bennis

> *"The End of Leadership — Leadership cannot exist without the full inclusion, initiatives, and the cooperation of employees."*

#### Team Decision-Making

**3 Main Areas:**
1. **Environments** — Thinking Environment (10 features: Attention, Equality, Appreciation 5:1, Ease, Encouragement, Feelings, Information, Place, Diversity, Incisive Questions)
2. **Methods** — Consensus → Majority Vote → Minority Vote → Authority after discussion → Averaging → Expert → Authority without discussion
3. **Tools** — Brainstorming, Affinity Grouping, Multi-voting, Criteria Matrix, Six Thinking Hats

#### Six Thinking Hats — MEMORIZE
| Hat | Meaning |
|-----|---------|
| ⬜ White | Data & facts |
| 🔴 Red | Feelings & intuition |
| ⬛ Black | Risks & problems |
| 🟡 Yellow | Benefits & value |
| 🔵 Blue | Process management |
| 🟢 Green | Creativity & new ideas |

#### Model Paper Q1 Case Study Points (NextGen Lanka)
- **Issue:** Sanduni (leader) makes unilateral decisions → no team meetings → junior members excluded
- **Effects:** Low motivation, weak collaboration, delays, trust breakdown
- **Fix:** Apply Situational Leadership — use Supportive/Participative style
- **Improvement Plan:** Introduce structured team meetings, use consensus decision-making, apply Brainstorming + Criteria Matrix for tech decisions

### 2. Professional Ethics & Codes of Conduct (Lecture 9) — Q2(a)

**Why critical:** Q2(a) explicitly asks to "explain professional ethics and the purpose of a code of conduct in IT."

#### Key Definitions
- **Ethics:** A set of beliefs about right or wrong behavior; conforms to generally accepted social norms
- **Professional Ethics:** Personal, organizational, and corporate standards expected of professionals
- **Code of Ethics:** Covers issues involving responsibilities to employees, employers, users, and the public

#### Professional Bodies & Codes (IT)
| Body | Key Points |
|------|-----------|
| **CSSL** | Professionalism, Professional Development, Honesty, Competence, Added Value to Sri Lanka |
| **ACM** | Public wellbeing, avoid harm, be honest, fair, honour IP/privacy/confidentiality |
| **IEEE** | Safety & welfare, avoid conflicts of interest, be honest, reject bribery |
| **ACM-SE (Software Engineering)** | 8 principles: Public, Client/Employer, Product, Judgment, Management, Profession, Colleagues, Self |

#### 5 Primary Functions of Codes of Ethics
1. **Inspire** — motivate ethical behavior
2. **Educate** — teach members about standards
3. **Guide** — help resolve dilemmas
4. **Responsibilities** — clarify duties
5. **Discipline** — enforce standards

#### IT Professional Commitments
- Integrity | Competence | Professional Responsibility | Work Responsibility | Societal Responsibility

### 3. Diversity & Inclusion (Lecture 7) — Q2(b)(c)(d)

**Why critical:** Q2(b)(c)(d) directly test this lecture's content.

#### Key Definition
> *"Inclusion is a sense of belonging. Inclusive cultures make people feel respected and valued for who they are as an individual or group."*

#### Types of Team Diversity
- Religious | Gender | Age | Race | National Culture | Disabilities | Personality (Intro/Extrovert)

#### Benefits of Diversity
1. Wider talent pool & skills
2. Creates innovation
3. Multiple perspectives → better decisions
4. Language skills open doors
5. Improves employee performance & retention

#### Challenges of Diversity
- Discrimination | Stereotyping | High turnover | Absenteeism | Team conflict | Tension | Productivity decrease

| Area | Effect of Exclusion |
|------|---------------------|
| **Employee Motivation** | Disengagement, low morale, reduced effort, sense of not belonging |
| **Teamwork** | Weak collaboration, silos, poor communication between groups |
| **Communication** | Distrust, information hoarding, conflict avoidance rather than resolution |

#### How to Overcome Diversity Challenges
1. **Planning & Implementation** — map diversity strategy
2. **Conflict Resolution Skills** — handle promptly
3. **Communication** — encourage open dialogue

#### Inclusion Connects to Team Models
- **Tuckman's Stages:** Inclusion accelerates Norming & Performing
- **Belbin Roles:** Inclusive environments allow all roles to thrive

### 4. Data Privacy & Legal Responsibility (Lecture 10) — Q3(a)(b)(d)

**Why critical:** Q3(a)(b)(d) directly test this.

#### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
- EU law (2018), covers 447 million people
- **Applies to non-EU companies** handling EU data (including Sri Lankan companies!)
- Heavy fines: **€20 million OR 4% of global annual revenue** (whichever is higher)
- Google fined €50M | Amazon fined €746M

#### GDPR User Rights
| Right | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Right to Access | Users can see what data is held and why |
| Right to Correction | Fix incorrect/outdated data |
| Right to Deletion (Right to Be Forgotten) | Erase data when no longer needed |
| Right to Data Portability | Transfer data in machine-readable format |

#### Data Flow Principles (MEMORIZE)
**Collect → Store → Use → Share → Delete**
**Consent → Security → Purpose → Transparency → Right to Erase**

#### Privacy Principles
- Collect only what is necessary (Data Minimization)
- Use only for stated purpose
- Delete when no longer needed
- Keep users informed (Transparency)

#### Sri Lankan Laws
| Law | Purpose |
|-----|---------|
| **PDPA No.9 of 2022** | Consent required; users can request deletion/correction; organizations must secure data |
| Computer Crimes Act (2007) | Criminal offenses for hacking |
| Electronic Transactions Act (2006) | Legitimizes digital signatures |
| Right to Information Act (2016) | Citizens can request public data |

#### Cambridge Analytica Case Study (Key Example)
- Facebook harvested 87M users' data without consent
- Used to influence US elections & Brexit
- Ethical violations: privacy, consent, manipulation
- Fix: strict data access limits, audits, strong consent, transparent communication

### 5. Intellectual Property (Lecture 13) — Q3(c)

**Why critical:** Q3(c) explicitly asks about IP risks in software development.

#### Types of IP Protection
| Type | Protects | Term |
|------|---------|------|
| **Patent** | Inventions (new, inventive, industrially applicable) | 20 years |
| **Copyright** | Expression of ideas (books, software, art) | Life + 70 years |
| **Trademark** | Brand names, logos, symbols | 10 years (renewable) |
| **Trade Secret** | Confidential commercial information | No limit |
| **Industrial Design** | Ornamental aspects (shapes, patterns) | Varies |

#### Patentability Criteria (TRIPS Article 27)
1. **Novelty** — not anticipated/known
2. **Inventive Step** — not obvious
3. **Industrial Applicability** — useful

#### Non-Patentable
- Mathematical formulae | Laws of nature | Naturally occurring organisms | Abstract ideas | Natural phenomena

#### Software Piracy Types (5)
1. **Soft-lifting** — one copy, multiple installs
2. **Client-Server Overuse** — exceed license count
3. **Internet Piracy** — download from unofficial sources
4. **Hard-Disk Loading** — pre-install on sold computers
5. **Software Counterfeiting** — illegal reproduction/distribution

#### IP Risks Without Permission (Q3c focus)
- **For Organizations:** Legal lawsuits, financial penalties, reputational damage, product recalls
- **For Individual Developers:** Criminal liability, job loss, fines, career damage

#### Sri Lankan IP Law: Act No. 36 of 2003
- Covers: copyrights, patents, industrial designs, trademarks, unfair competition, trade secrets
- NIPO — National Intellectual Property Office

### 6. Sustainability & CSR (Lectures 11 & 12) — Q4

**Why critical:** Q4 is built entirely from these two lectures.

#### Sustainability Definition
> *"Sustainability means meeting the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs."*

#### Triple Bottom Line
- **People** (Social) | **Planet** (Environment) | **Profit** (Economic)

#### Environmental Issues in IT
| Issue | Details |
|-------|---------|
| **E-Waste** | Rapid hardware obsolescence, hazardous materials |
| **Energy/Carbon Emissions** | Data centers (AWS, Google), cryptocurrency mining |
| **Software Inefficiency** | Increases energy consumption |
| **Fast Upgrade Culture** | Phones every 1-2 years → waste |

#### Solutions (Green Computing)
- Energy-efficient software/hardware
- Circular economy (repair, refurbish, recycle)
- Align with UN SDGs
- Encourage repair culture

#### Carroll's Four Responsibilities of Business (CSR)
| Level | Type | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| 1 | **Economic** | Produce goods/services of value, repay creditors, increase shareholder wealth |
| 2 | **Legal** | Obey laws defined by governments |
| 3 | **Ethical** | Follow generally held beliefs about behavior |
| 4 | **Discretionary** | Purely voluntary (e.g., daycare, charitable work) |

#### Digital Inclusion — Definition & Components
> *"Ensuring everyone, regardless of age, gender, income, disability, or location, can access and effectively use digital technologies."*

**5 A's Components:**
1. **Access** — availability of internet & devices
2. **Affordability** — cost barriers
3. **Ability** — digital literacy & skills
4. **Accessibility** — inclusive design for disabilities
5. **Awareness** — understanding digital benefits

#### Impact of Lack of Digital Inclusion (Q4c)
- Education gaps (no e-learning access)
- Economic exclusion (no digital banking/services)
- Social isolation
- Health inequalities (no telemedicine)

#### Sri Lankan Digital Divide
- Urban vs Rural areas
- English vs Sinhala/Tamil speakers
- Younger vs Older populations
- 2.7 billion people still offline globally

### 7. Ethical Decision-Making Models (Lecture 10)

| Model | Principle | Strength | Weakness |
|-------|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Utilitarianism** | Choose what benefits most people | Good for large-scale decisions | Can harm minorities |
| **Deontology** | Some actions are always right/wrong (rule-based) | Protects rights absolutely | Ignores consequences |
| **Stakeholder Analysis** | Consider all affected parties | Inclusive, comprehensive | Complex to implement |

**Key Application:** Cambridge Analytica — Utilitarianism: harm > benefit; Deontology: privacy violation = always wrong.

### 8. Power & Organizational Politics (Lecture 8)

#### French & Raven's 5 Forms of Power
| Power | Definition | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Reward** | Control over rewards target wants | Bonuses, promotions |
| **Legitimate** | Right based on position | Manager enforcing policies |
| **Coercive** | Ability to cause unpleasant experience | Disciplinary action |
| **Expert** | Has knowledge target needs | IT specialist's technical advice |
| **Referent (Charismatic)** | Based on interpersonal attraction | Popular/inspiring leader |

**Power vs Authority:** Power = ability to influence | Authority = right to influence

#### Organizational Culture
- Pattern of basic assumptions learned by a group to cope with external adaptation & internal integration
- **6 Characteristics:** Behavioral regularities, Norms, Dominant values, Philosophy, Rules, Organizational climate

### 10. Discrimination & Stereotyping (Lecture 7)

- **Discrimination** — unfair treatment based on protected characteristics (age, gender, race, disability, religion, pregnancy, sexual orientation)
- **Stereotyping** — oversimplified attitude toward those outside one's own experience
- **Prejudice** — subjective, often derogatory assumptions

**Protected Characteristics (UK Equality Act):** Age, gender, race, disability, religion, pregnancy & maternity, sexual orientation, gender reassignment, marriage & civil partnership

### 11. Cultural Dimensions (Lecture 7)

- **High-context cultures** — words convey limited part of message (Sri Lanka, China, Arab countries, Japan)
- **Low-context cultures** — message conveyed directly by words (USA, Germany, Canada, Scandinavia)
- **Polychronic** — circular view of time (present-focused)
- **Monochronic** — linear view (future-planning focused)
- **Proxemics** — study of how people use space (varies by culture)

## 📝 Practice Scenario Questions

**Scenario 1 (Q1 type):** *A project manager at a Sri Lankan software firm always decides alone without consulting the team. Junior members feel excluded. Delays are common. What leadership issues exist? What style should be applied?*
