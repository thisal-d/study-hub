## Delivery Structure:

Lectures: 2 hours weekly - Number of sessions depends on the total number of students. 240/500 students per session.

Case Study Discussions: 2 hours weekly in sub-batches. Each 240- student class is divided into:

Batch A: 120 students (60 students $ \times $2 groups) presenting per week. Each group consists of 6 members.

Batch B: 60 students peer evaluating, alternating weekly.

Each batch explores a unique case study designed around the same lecture topic for that week.

## Overview of IT2160 Learning Structure

IT2160 combines:

- Lectures: Introduce concepts and theoretical frameworks.

- Case Studies: Apply theories to real-world scenarios.

- Peer Evaluations: Build judgement and feedback skills.

- Group Activities: Build communication and teamwork skills.

- Reflective Practice: Anchor learning to personal development.

## Introduction to Case Studies

Case studies develop:

- Critical thinking

- Real-life problem solving

- Professional communication

- Ethical reasoning

- Teamwork and conflict resolution

They simulate the experiences of real-world IT analysts,managers, and engineers.

## Case Study Workflow

The structured cycle:

1. Read and analyze the case deeply

2. Discuss insights with your team

3. Build slides and arguments

4. Present to class (professional delivery)

5. Receive & give peer evaluations

6. Reflect and improve

This model's industry project cycles.

## Peer Evaluation

Peer evaluation helps students:

- Practice constructive criticism

- Recognize strengths and improvement areas

- Build accountability

- Experience professional review mechanisms

- Develop fair and objective judgment

## Mini Workshop: How to Analyze a Case Study

A strong case analysis:

- Identifies the real underlying problem (not just symptoms)

- Considers human, organizational, technical, and ethical dimensions

- Applies module concepts

- Evaluates multiple solution paths

- Justifies recommendations with evidence

## Mini Workshop: How to Present a Case Study

A strong presentation focuses on:

- Storytelling structure

- Clear flow of logic

- Professional speaking and formatting

- Equal team participation

- Prepared answers for questions

## Introducing Personal Development Planning (PDP)

PDP builds structured personal learning cycles:

- It encourages students to consider who they are, where they want to go, and how they plan to get there.

- Helps articulate short- and long-term goals.

- Encourages proactive career planning (CV building, internships, certifications).

- Builds academic maturity through planned learning strategies.

- PDP as "a conscious and systematic process of improvement."

## Four Stages of PDP

## Reflection

- Where am I now?

- Draw from past experiences, self-assessment tools, and feedback.

## Planning

- Where do I want to go?

- Set SMART goals: specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, time-bound Action

- What will I do?

- Implement steps (courses, projects, habits, skill-building).

- Review

- How did I do?

- Evaluate progress, identify obstacles, adjust plans.
