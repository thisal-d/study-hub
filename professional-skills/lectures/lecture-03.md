## 1. Economic Capital- "What You Have ACCESS To"

Economic capital refers to the physical and financial resources that support learning, but these are NOT owned by students.

At SLIIT, these include:

- Computer labs and hardware

- Licensed software

- Networking and cybersecurity labs

- High-speed internet and Wi-Fi

- Library facilities and digital repositories

- Campus infrastructure, classrooms, equipment

Access to SLIIT's resources creates opportunities for your growth

## 2. Human Capital- "What You Learn and Become Skilled At"

This is the knowledge and abilities you personally build through academic work.

Examples for computing students:

Programming languages

Software development

Databases and SQL

Networks and cybersecurity

Cloud computing

Data structures and algorithms

Problem-solving and analytical thinking

Human capital grows through your effort and practice.

## 3. Social Capital-“The People You Connect With at SLIIT”

This is the network and relationships you build throughout your degree. Examples:

Friends in your batch

Teammates from group projects

Lecturers, advisors

Senior students and alumni

Industry exposure from SLIIT events, Hackathons, CodeFests, ICAC

This capital provides you with support, opportunities, and connections that benefit you long after graduation.

## 4. Psychological Capital (PsyCap) - "Who YOU Are as a Person and Professional"

This is your mindset, attitude, and inner strengths.

It includes:

Confidence: belief in your abilities

Hope: clear goals and pathways

Optimism: positive attitude towards outcomes

Resilience: ability to bounce back from setbacks

PsyCap is built through real challenges, exams, retakes, group conflicts, deadlines, presentations, and personal reflection.
